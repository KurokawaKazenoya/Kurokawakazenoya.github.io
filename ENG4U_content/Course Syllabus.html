Course OverviewAssignments

This course emphasizes the consolidation of the literacy, communication, and critical and creative thinking skills necessary for success in academic and daily life. Students will analyse a range of challenging literary texts from various periods, countries, and cultures; interpret and evaluate informational and graphic texts; and create oral, written, and media texts in a variety of forms. An important focus will be on using academic language coherently and confidently, selecting the reading strategies best suited to particular texts and particular purposes for reading, and developing greater control in writing. The course is intended to prepare students for university, college, or the workplace.

Unit 1: Identity

Overview


In this unit, students embark on a profound exploration of personal and cultural identity through literature and reflective activities. Beginning with defining identity complexities and creating personal identity collages, students delve into the challenges of self-discovery and belonging in diverse societies, analyzing the impact of societal pressures and norms on individuality. Engaging with literary works and visual representations, they explore the intricate intersections of race, gender, sexuality, and socioeconomic background, fostering a deep understanding of identity while honing their analytical and reflective skills within the context of English language arts standards.

Lessons

Lesson 1: Students will define and explore the concept of personal and cultural identity.

Lesson 2: Students will examine the challenges individuals face in finding their identity and a sense of belonging in diverse societies.

Lesson 3: Students will analyze literary works that delve into the concept of self-discovery, acceptance, and the impact.

Lesson 4: Students will analyze and discuss the impact of race, gender, sexuality, and socioeconomic background on personal and cultural identity.

Lesson 5: Students will reflect on their learning and create a personal identity statement.

  • Poetry: Totem Poles & Railroads
  • Podcast: Pieces Episode 1-5
  • Article: It isn’t easy being me

Unit 3: Impact

Overview

In this unit, students explore the intricate web of ecological challenges and climate change. They begin by delving into foundational concepts, examining the impact of human activities on the environment, and considering diverse viewpoints on climate change. The exploration continues with an analysis of literature addressing environmental issues, fostering critical thinking through discussions and reflective papers that delve into the consequences of environmental degradation. Students also explore the vital realm of conservation and environmental stewardship, studying various strategies and real-life success stories. Ethical considerations come into focus as students investigate different ethical frameworks related to the environment, analyzing literature that raises ethical questions and discussing the role of literature in ecological awareness. The unit culminates in synthesizing knowledge, encouraging reflective discussions, and nurturing creative and critical thinking about humanity's intricate relationship with the natural world, fostering a holistic understanding of environmental sustainability. Through engaging discussions, analyses, and reflections, students develop a nuanced perspective on the complex interplay between environmental issues, society, and literature.

Lessons

Lesson 1: Students will understand the concept of ecological challenges and climate change.

Lesson 2: Students will analyze literature that explores environmental issues.

Lesson 3: Students will examine the importance of conservation and environmental stewardship.

Lesson 4: Students will engage in discussions on environmental ethics and eco-criticism.

Lesson 5: Students will synthesize their learning and reflect on humanity's relationship with the natural world.

Texts

  • Novel Excerpts: The Carbon Diaries
  • Poetry: Webcam the world
  • Podcast: How to Save a Planet
  • Film: This Changes Everything

Unit 4: Power

Overview

In this multifaceted exploration, students delve into the influential realm of banned books, dissecting their impact on individuals and societies. They begin by understanding the concept of banned literature, analyzing the reasons behind censorship, and exploring the far-reaching implications for both individuals and communities. Moving forward, students examine the narratives of banned books, identifying themes and messages that challenge societal norms. They further investigate specific examples from these books, using them to create visual representations that illustrate the transformative power of storytelling in shaping perceptions and questioning established norms. The historical and contemporary contexts of banned books are scrutinized, revealing literature's pivotal role in driving social change. Culminating in a creative endeavor, students craft their own banned book, incorporating compelling elements that challenge norms and contribute meaningfully to the discourse. Through critical discussions, analyses, and creative pursuits, students gain a profound understanding of literature's capacity to challenge, inspire, and catalyze societal transformation. Assessment methods encompass diverse tasks, including journal entries, research presentations, and the creation of original banned book excerpts, ensuring a comprehensive exploration of this impactful subject.

Lessons

Lesson 1: Students will analyze the impact of literature on individuals and societies by examining the concept of banned books.

Lesson 2: Students will explore the power of storytelling by examining the narratives of banned books.

Lesson 3:  Students will examine how stories shape perceptions and challenge norms by analyzing specific examples from banned books.

Lesson 4: Students will investigate how literature and storytelling contribute to social change by examining the historical and contemporary context of banned books.

Lesson 5: Students will synthesize their understanding of the power of literature and storytelling by creating their own banned book.

Texts

  • Novel Excerpts: Handmaid’s Tale, 1984
  • News: Banned Speech
  • Podcast: Banned Book Club
  • Film: ABCs of Book Banning

Unit 5: Independent Novel Study

Overview

In this culminating task, students will choose a novel from a list of pre-approved literature or propose one for approval by the instructor. They will read and analyze the novel independently, paying attention to themes, character development, and the author's style, writing a comprehensive report that includes a summary, analysis, and your personal reflections on the book and consider how it connects to the course themes.

Banned Book Adaptation 15%

Task: Choose a banned book and adapt it into a different medium, such as a short film, podcast, graphic novel, or interactive website. Justify creative choices, emphasizing the essence of the story, characters, and themes. Present the adaptation to the class, discussing the challenges faced and the artistic liberties taken to maintain the integrity of the original work while adapting it for a new medium.

Literary Analysis Essay 20%

Task: Choose one of the works studied during the course (novel, play, or poem) and write a literary analysis essay. Explore a specific aspect of the work, such as its symbolism, narrative structure, or thematic elements. Support your analysis with evidence from the text and critical perspectives. This assignment hones your ability to perform close readings and engage in scholarly analysis.



Exploring Dystopian or Utopian Futures 15%

Task: In this assignment, students are tasked with crafting a short story set in either a dystopian or utopian future where technology plays a pivotal role. Through their narratives, students will explore the consequences, challenges, and complexities faced by the characters in these futuristic worlds. Whether it's a society overrun by invasive technology or one thriving due to technological advancements, students are encouraged to delve deep into the human experience within these settings.



Argumentative Essay 20%

Task: Participate in a class debate on a controversial literary topic (e.g., censorship, authorial intent). Research your position thoroughly and construct persuasive arguments. After the debate, write an argumentative essay defending your position, incorporating class discussion points and additional research.
