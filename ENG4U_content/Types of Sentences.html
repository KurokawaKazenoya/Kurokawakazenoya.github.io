Types of Sentences by Function;

There are four types of sentences under this category:

  • Declarative;
  • Exclamatory
  • Interrogative
  • Imperative

Declarative

  • Declarative sentences are statements.
  • The function of this sentence is to state or describe something.
  • It ends with a period ( . )Examples:
  • They thought of travelling to Europe for their vacation. 
  • I finished the movie before they arrived.

Exclamatory

  • These are sentences that show strong emotion.
  • This usually ends with an exclamation point ( ! )
  • Examples:
  • “Here’s Johnny!”
  • “It’s alive! It’s alive!”
  • Try writing your own sentence!

Interrogative

  • Interrogative sentences are essentially questions.
  • They end in a question mark (?).
  • Examples:
  • “Are you talking to me?”
  • “When will you come see me?”
  • Try writing your own sentence!

Imperative

  • When someone commands or requests you to do something, the statement they give is called an imperative sentence.
  • Imperative sentences end with either an exclamation point or period so you better read the sentences carefully to check its meaning!
  • Examples:
  • “Show me the money!”
  • “Make him an offer he can’t refuse.”
  • Try writing your own sentence!

Now that you know the types of sentences by function, let’s now explore the types of sentences by structure.

First, we have to understand the concept of clauses.

CLAUSES

  • Clauses are combinations of words and phrases that give either a complete or incomplete thought.
  • Usually, you know it is a clause when a subject and a verb appear together.

"I’ll have what she’s having because that looks tasty."

INDEPENDENT CLAUSE DEPENDENT CLAUSE

“I’ll have what she’s having.”

  

It is made up of a subject and a predicate and it can stand alone. 

“Because that looks tasty.”

It cannot stand alone due to the subordinating word “because”

Simple Sentence

  • This type contains 1 independent clause. 
  • It expresses a complete thought.
  • Examples:
  • You know how to whistle.
  • The group will need a bigger boat.
  • Try writing your own simple sentence!

Compound Sentence

  • A compound sentence is a sentence that has at least two independent clauses joined by either a semicolon, comma, or a conjunction.
  • Examples:
  • We don’t need badges, and we don’t have to show you any badges.
  • Today I consider myself the luckiest man on the face of the earth; I just got my dream job!
  • Try writing your own compound sentence!

Complex Sentence

  • If the sentence contains one independent clause and at least one dependent clause, this sentence is a complex sentence.
  • Examples:
  • Because I left the chocolates in the park, I will have to go back to the park.
  • If you build it, he will come.
  • Try writing your own complex sentence!

Compound-Complex Sentence

  • Basically, this is a combination of a compound and complex sentence.
  • It has at least two independent clauses and at least one dependent clause.
  • Examples:
  • Even though nobody’s perfect, you still want to strive for excellence, and you have to give it your all.
  • Try writing your own compound-complex sentence!
