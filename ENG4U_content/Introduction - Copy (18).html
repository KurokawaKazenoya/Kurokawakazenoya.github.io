End of Play Tasks

Lesson Description

Students will work on two tasks that summarize the main events of Hamlet. The first has students organize summaries of each act, and to present it in a manner of their choosing (creativity is encouraged). The second task is to choose a soliloquy of Hamlet’s to thoroughly analyze and write a response to it. 

Overall Expectations

B1 Reading for Meaning: read and demonstrate an understanding of a variety of literary, informational, and graphic texts, using a range of strategies to construct meaning

B2 Understanding Form and Style: recognize a variety of text forms, text features, and stylistic elements and demonstrate an understanding of how they help communicate meaning; 

C1 Developing and Organizing Content: generate, gather, and organize ideas and information to write for an intended purpose and audience.

C2 Using Knowledge of Form and Style: draft and revise their writing, using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms and stylistic elements appropriate for the purpose and audience; 

C3 Applying Knowledge of Conventions: use editing, proofreading, publishing skills and strategies, and knowledge of language conventions to correct errors, refine expression, and present their work effectively.

Learning Goals

  • Students will identify key aspects from each Act of Hamlet as an act of summarizing the complex text.
  • Students will choose a soliloquy from Hamlet to thoroughly analyze.
  • Students will create a literary analysis in response to the chosen soliloquy.

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

  • I can identify key aspects from each Act of Hamlet as an act of summarizing the complex text.
  • I can choose a soliloquy from Hamlet to thoroughly analyze.
  • I can create a literary analysis in response to the chosen soliloquy.
