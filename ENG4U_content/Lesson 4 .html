Lesson 4

A profound exploration of technology's impact on personal lives. Beginning with introspective journaling, students reflect on their digital habits, cultivating self-awareness. Engaging discussions follow, delving into the complexities of digital identity, fostering critical thinking about online and offline balance. Further, students analyze media content, discerning the impact of social platforms on identity, honing research skills. Simultaneously, linguistic mastery is refined as students differentiate and apply gerunds and infinitives, ensuring grammatical accuracy. Literary analysis gains depth as students integrate terms like irony and symbolism into their writing, enhancing their ability to dissect literature. Through this multifaceted approach, students emerge with heightened self-awareness, critical thinking skills, and refined language and literary abilities.

What's the Big Idea?

Students will reflect on their own relationship with technology and the blurred lines between the digital and physical worlds.

Success Criteria

  • Integrate literary terms such as irony, foreshadowing, and symbolism within sentences and paragraphs
  • Apply knowledge of gerunds and infinitives in sentences
  • Investigate the impact of social media and online platforms on personal identity
