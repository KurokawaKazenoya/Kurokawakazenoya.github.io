FEMINISM

Remember Metamorphosis? Here are some things to consider: 

In a domestic setting where men and women are contending for respect and power, the women are portrayed as either inferior and weak or valued only for aesthetic pleasure. 

Grete and her mother are marginalized or regarded as ‘others’ by the male family members. Her father and brother dominate Grete. She does not get any scope to work outside or live independently. After Gregor’s transformation into a bug, Grete gets the scope to support the family and prove her individuality. But her family members see it from another feminist perspective that Grete is grown up enough to be married off. She becomes a commodity in the marriage market.

MARXISM

Gregor Samsa, the Worker 

It is no surprise how, even in the first few novella pages, the class relations are established.

Gregor Samsa, the protagonist, signifies the proletariat, or the working class, and his unnamed manager represents the bourgeoisie. 

His metamorphosis into a "monstrous vermin" serves as a metaphor for any type of impairment that renders the worker unable to work. 

Gregor's value as a laborer diminishes when he is unable to work. After he has lost his worth, his manager, family and even his life abandon him and leave him to a miserable, solitary death.

HISTORICAL

Though the novel is fictional, Lord of the Flies' exploration of the idea of human evil is at least partly based on Golding’s experience with the real-life violence and brutality of World War II. Free from the rules and structures of civilization and society, the boys on the island in Lord of the Flies descent into savagery. 

 

As the boys splinter into factions, some behave peacefully and work together to maintain order and achieve common goals, while others rebel and seek only anarchy and violence. 

MORAL/DIDACTIC

In Lord of the Flies, William Golding sees moral behavior, in many cases, as something that civilization forces upon the individual rather than a natural expression of human individuality. 

 

When left to their own devices, Golding implies, people naturally revert to cruelty, savagery, and barbarism. Although, not as straightforward in providing a lesson, the readers are presented a view into the human soul.

PSYCHOANALYTIC

Throughout the novel Lord of the Flies, the three boys Jack, Ralph, and Piggy, are used to explore the three different types of personalities. Jack Merridew’s impulsive and sexual actions demonstrate the id, Ralph trying to keep the peace displays ego, and Piggy’s overly judgmental self is used to explore the superego. 

 

In their isolation, each child’s new personality arises as a devastating effect of their separation and loneliness.
