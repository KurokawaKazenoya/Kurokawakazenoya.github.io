Types of Essay

Keeping the purpose in mind, let’s look at the six types of essays.

The EXPOSITORY Essay (EXPLANATION)

  • This essay describes or explains a topic.
  • For example, “How to Teach a Cat How to Play Fetch”, written in paragraphs, would work.

The PERSUASIVE/OPINION Essay (DEFEND AN ARGUMENT)

  • The author chooses a side and attempts to convince readers to agree with their thesis.

The NARRATIVE Essay (RECOUNT A STORY)

  • This essay tells a story in an informal manner. It could be about the author’s childhood, someone’s views on an issue, etc.
  • The essay is written in a way that “talks” to the readers.

The REVIEW (EVALUATE SOMETHING)

  • This type of essay takes something apart, (such as a film, book, or play), and analyzes it.
  • For example, a review could cover the plot, conflict, and characters of a novel by explaining what happens, what worked, and what did not, and ultimately provide a recommendation for the audience.

The RESEARCH Essay (CONNECTING IDEAS)

  • These essays involve referencing different sources, (other books, reports, etc.), that will support the writer’s thesis by supporting and connecting to the writer’s own ideas.

The LITERARY Essay (EXPLORING LITERATURE)

  • A literary essay explores the meaning and construction of a novel, story, play, poem, etc.
  • Instead of simply being a review, the writer argues for the “point” of the text by providing direct support and explanations.
  • This could include commenting on parts of a text such as conflict, character, theme, etc., and should be supported with direct references to the piece of literature.
