Literary Essay

Let’s focus on the literary essay and go through the process: step-by-step.

Literary Essay Topic
Choose one of the topics indicated in your Unit 3 Summative.

INTRODUCTION BODY CONCLUSION

For the introduction, you need to follow this structure:

  • LEAD
  • A lead’s function is to capture the interest of your reader.
  • It is a general statement about the topic.
  • It does not mention the literary work.
  • At this level, it should be 2-3 sentences.
  • LINK
  • The link connects the lead to the thesis.
  • It is a transitional statement.
  • It should be 1 sentence.
  • It should mention the name of the literary work OR the author.
  • Try not to use both, as it is too repetitive.
  • Whichever one you choose, use the other in your thesis.
  • THESIS
  • This is the statement that you will be arguing.
  • It MUST be an opinion-something that can be argued-has two sides.
  • It is NOT a fact-based statement.
  • It should be 1 sentence.
  • BLUEPRINT
  • This refers to your three support topics. What reasons do you have to support your thesis?
  • The blueprint can be 3 sentences or 1-this depends on how detailed your items are.
  • The blueprint MUST use parallel structure.
  • Parallel structure refers to using the same grammatical structure throughout the statement.

The body paragraphs follow this structure:
Yes, you read it right, it’s paragraphs because there can be more than one body paragraph.

  • TOPIC SENTENCE
  • A topic sentence follows a formula-if you use the right items, you will successfully write a topic sentence.
  • Topic Sentence = Transition + Thesis + Blueprint
  • POINT
  • After the topic sentence, write one sentence that depicts your first point.
  • This is an overview-you are not to explain it.
  • This should be 1 sentence.
  • Do not blend this into your quote.
  • PROOF
  • Use a quotation from the literary work to support your point.
  • The quotation must be introduced with contextual clues (speaker tags, references to events).
  • Use the correct punctuation to introduce a quotation.
  • COMMENT
  • This is the most important portion of your essay.
  • This deals with your ability to provide analysis, critical thinking, and insight.
  • Your comment should be 4 sentences.
  • At this level, there should be evidence of critical thinking (deep analysis and insight into the literary work)
  • Use words from the quotation to start your comment.
  • Complete your body paragraph by writing your comment on the essay topic.

The concluding paragraph follows this structure:

  • CONCLUDING SENTENCE
  • Recap the paragraph
  • Go back to your topic sentence and recap the thesis and blueprint
  • Begin the concluding sentence with a transition (therefore, moreover, furthermore, hence)
  • CONCLUSION
  • This is where you state your main answer to the question.
  • THESIS RESTATEMENT
  • Using different wording, restate your thesis.
  • Change the wording so that your essay is not boring.
  • BLUEPRINT RESTATEMENT
  • Using different wording, restate your blueprint.
  • CLINCHER
  • Picking up the idea that you used in your lead, write a general, thought-provoking clincher
  • This should be 3-4 sentences and it should further develop the concept you discussed in your lead.
  • A good clincher causes the reader to think about your essay after putting it down.

Follow this process and you’ll complete your literary essay!

But before anything else, here are some guidelines to follow:

Open All Panels

WRITE IN THE PRESENT TENSE

  • Fortunato likes to drink fine wine.  
  • NOT Fortunato liked to drink fine wine.

DO NOT USE CONTRACTIONS

  • Write — does not
    NOT doesn’t
  • Remember — cannot is 1 word!!!

USE THE ACTIVE VOICE

  • Fortunato likes to drink fine wine. = active
  • Drinking fine wine is liked by Fortunato. = passive

USE HIGHER LEVEL VOCABULARY

  • Avoid slang or colloquial expressions.
  • Avoid the words ‘good, bad, thing, a lot, nice’ as they are weak.
  • Check diction=use words that are strong.
  • Caution-avoid overuse of the thesaurus or your essay will not make sense.

Use transitions to make your writing flow smoothly.

Use a variety of sentence structures. (simple, compound, complex, compound/complex)
Add colour to your writing by adding rhetorical devices (simile, metaphor, allusion, etc.) Try to add one devicce per body paragraph.
Never use personal pronouns in an essay
I, me, we, our, mine, myself, you, your, etc.
Use third-person pronouns
he, she, it, one, them, their, they, him, her, his, hers, etc.
Never use phrases such as, ‘this essay will prove’, ‘this quotation proves’, or ‘this proves’.
