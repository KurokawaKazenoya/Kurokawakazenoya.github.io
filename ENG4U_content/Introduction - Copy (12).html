Introduction Hamlet & Dramatic Significance

Lesson Description

Students will be introduced to the play Hamlet by Shakespeare, particularly with the characters, setting, and themes that will be discussed. For this unit, the text studied will be in modernized language so that students can focus on dramatic significance, character development, irony, and figurative language (i.e. allusions, hyperbole). After the lesson students will do a short research task to further their knowledge of Shakespearean life.

Overall Expectations

B1. Reading for Meaning: read and demonstrate an understanding of a variety of literary, informational, and graphic texts, using a range of strategies to construct meaning;

B2. Understanding Form and Style: recognize a variety of text forms, text features, and stylistic elements and demonstrate an understanding of how they help communicate meaning.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 read a variety of student- and teacher-selected texts from diverse cultures and historical periods, identifying specific purposes for reading

B1.6 analyze texts in terms of the information, ideas, issues, or themes they explore, examining how various aspects of the texts contribute to the presentation or development of these elements

B2.1 identify a variety of characteristics of literary, informational, and graphic text forms and demonstrate insight into the way they help communicate meaning

B2.2 identify a variety of text features and demonstrate insight into the way they communicate meaning.

Learning Goals

  • Students will learn about the background history of Hamlet by Shakespeare.
  • Students will look at the scrutiny and criticism surrounding the play.
  • Students will learn about the characters, setting, and themes of Hamlet in this introduction.
  • Students will define key terms for Shakespearean tragedies.

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

  • I can understand facts about the background history of Hamlet by Shakespeare.
  • I can understand recent scrutiny and criticism surrounding the play.
  • I can begin to identify the characters, setting, and themes of Hamlet.
  • I can define key terms for Shakespearean tragedies.
