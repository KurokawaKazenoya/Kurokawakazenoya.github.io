Lesson 1

Students embark on a captivating introduction to the unit, delving into a discussion on the pivotal role of technology in society. Through lively class discussions, students explore the intricate tapestry of technology's impact on relationships, examining both its positive and negative effects.

The lesson deepens as students critically read and analyze articles or case studies, immersing themselves in real-life examples that illuminate the complexities of technology's influence. Furthermore, students participate in reflective discussions, sharing their personal experiences with technology and relationships, fostering a deeper understanding of the topic. Concurrently, the lesson hones their writing skills: students master cohesive devices, including transitional words, phrases, and clauses, ensuring seamless paragraph and essay structures.

Additionally, they refine their literary analysis techniques, focusing on paragraph development in essays. Emphasizing key elements such as topic sentences, textual evidence, and analysis, students learn to construct compelling and coherent arguments, enriching both their analytical and writing abilities in the process.

    

What's the Big Idea?

Students will examine the impact of technology on human relationships.

Success Criteria

  • Analyze the societal impact of technology, distinguishing between positive and negative effects on relationship
  • Identify and explain cohesive devices in written texts, recognizing transitional words, phrases, and clauses that enhance paragraph and essay structure,
  • Apply an understanding of paragraph development in literary analysis, integrating clear topic sentences, relevant textual evidence, and insightful analysis
