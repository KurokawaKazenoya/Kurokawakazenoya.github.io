Research Introduction

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will be introduced to the importance of research. Students will learn about qualitative and quantitative research. They will be given guidelines on how to choose their own research topic, which will be the primary focus of this unit.

Overall Expectations

C1. Developing and Organizing Content: generate, gather, and organize ideas and information to write for an intended purpose and audience.

Specific Expectations

C1.1 identify the topic, purpose, and audience for a variety of writing tasks

C1.2 generates, expands, explores, and focuses ideas for potential writing tasks, using a variety of strategies and print, electronic, and other resources, as appropriate.

C1.3 Locate and select information to fully and effectively support ideas for writing, using a variety of strategies and print, electronic, and other resources, as appropriate.

C1.4 identify, sort, and order main ideas and supporting details for writing tasks, using a variety of strategies and selecting the organizational pattern best suited to the content and the purpose of writing.

C1.5 Determine whether the ideas and information gathered are accurate and complete, interesting, and effectively meet the requirements of the writing task.

Learning Goals

  • Students will learn about research.
  • Students will differentiate research types.
  • Students will be guided in choosing their main research topic.

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

  • I can explain different aspects of research.
  • I can differentiate between research types.
  • I can choose my main research topic.
