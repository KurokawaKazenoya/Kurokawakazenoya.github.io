What Exactly is “Research”?

  • Deconstructing the word, we can identify that the prefix re- means again and search means to look for something.
  • Research formally means the systematic investigation of materials and concepts.
  • Most people think that research is only for new concepts but that’s rarely the case.
  • When we research, we don’t just focus on creating something new to make lives easier. We also seek out knowledge to improve existing research.

  

Two Types of Research:

 

Qualitative  Qualitative
  • Qualitative research is the process of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting non-numerical data such as language. 
  • Qualitative research can be used to understand how an individual subjectively perceives and gives meaning to their social reality.
  • Quantitative research involves the process of objectively collecting and analyzing numerical data to describe, predict, or control variables of interest. 
  • The goal of quantitative research is to test causal relationships.

Good Tactics for Choosing a Topic

Accordion

Good Tactics for Choosing a Topic: Something You Know

  • Primarily, when looking for a topic to research, you should stick to what you passionately know. 
  • Once you identified that, it’s easy to keep working on your research. 
  • Most people's tendency is that they choose topics that they don’t really care about, and in the end, they abandon their research.

Good Tactics for Choosing a Topic: Relevance

  • In choosing your topic for research, make sure that what you are working on has relevance. 
  • The goal is always to improve life → make things easier, fair and more ideal for everyone. 
  • We aim to understand and not just to prove something wrong.

Good Tactics for Choosing a Topic: Background Info

  • Know more about your topic!
  • Read background information and the current status of the topic at hand. 
  • It helps you figure out the appropriate approach for your research.
