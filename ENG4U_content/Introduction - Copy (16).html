Hamlet: Act IV

Lesson Description

Students will read aloud from the 7 scenes in Act 4 of Hamlet. Depending on the class size and comfort level, students can be assigned particular roles to read, or they may volunteer for them. Students will read aloud from the play, as it is broken up scene by scene. If students require clarification of what is happening/being said, the teacher will stop to explain the significance.

Overall Expectations

A1. Listening to Understand: listen in order to understand and respond appropriately in a variety of situations for a variety of purposes.

A2. Speaking to Communicate: use speaking skills and strategies appropriately to communicate with different audiences for a variety of purposes.

B1. Reading for Meaning: read and demonstrate an understanding of a variety of literary, informational, and graphic texts, using a range of strategies to construct meaning.

B2. Understanding Form and Style: recognize a variety of text forms, text features, and stylistic elements and demonstrate an understanding of how they help communicate meaning.

B3. Reading With Fluency: use knowledge of words and cueing systems to read fluently.

Specific Expectations

A1.4 identify the important information and ideas in oral texts, including complex and challenging texts, in a variety of ways

A1.5 develop and explain interpretations of oral texts, including complex and challenging texts, using evidence from the text and the oral and visual cues used in it insightfully to support

A2.3 communicate in a clear, coherent manner, using a structure and style effective for the purpose, subject matter, and intended audience

B1.3 identify the most important ideas and supporting details in texts, including complex and challenging texts

B1.6 analyze texts in terms of the information, ideas, issues, or themes they explore, examining how various aspects of the texts contribute to the presentation or development of these elements

B2.2 identify a variety of text features and demonstrate insight into the way they communicate meaning

B2.3 identify a variety of elements of style in texts and explain how they help communicate meaning and enhance the effectiveness of the texts

B3.1 automatically understands most words in a variety of reading contexts.

Learning Goals

  • Students will read aloud from the modern text of Hamlet.
  • Students will listen to others as they read the text.
  • Students will practice their fluency of reading.
  • Students will analyse the text and demonstrate their understanding of the events occurring in the play.

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

  • I can read aloud from the modern text of Hamlet.
  • I can listen to others as they read from the text.
  • I can practice my fluency of reading.
  • I can analyse the text and demonstrate my understanding of the events occurring in the play.
