  • Cultivation theory holds that long-term exposure to media shapes how media consumers perceive the world and conduct themselves. 
  • The cultivation hypothesis states that the more television people watch, the more likely they are to hold a view of reality closer to television's depiction

  • This means that people learn from observing other people’s actions.
  • Applying this to media means that people adapt and follow the behavior they view in media.

  • Symbolic interactionism is a theory that focuses on how individuals interact. 
  • It argues that people's actions are based on the meanings they assign to things, which can differ depending on the person and can change over time.
  • For Example: What does “Love” mean to you? Do you think your meaning of this will change? 
  • Critics say the theory is too narrow, but proponents claim it can help explain social change and society as a whole
