They begin by identifying various banned books, expanding their literary repertoire and cultural awareness. Analyzing the narrative elements of selected banned books, students delve deep into storytelling techniques, enhancing their understanding of literary craft. Engaging in thoughtful discussions, they explore the themes and messages conveyed in these books, delving into the societal, cultural, and political contexts that underpin these narratives. Additionally, they refine their language skills by mastering the transformation of direct speech into indirect (reported) speech, showcasing grammatical precision and effective communication. Further, their literary analysis skills flourish as they seamlessly integrate quotations into their analyses, enhancing the depth and persuasiveness of their interpretations. Through this comprehensive approach, students emerge not only with a nuanced understanding of banned literature but also with refined language proficiency, advanced analytical abilities, and persuasive communication skills, empowering them to engage critically and eloquently with complex literary themes and societal issues.
