Lesson 3

Exploring various conservation methods, students gain multifaceted perspectives on strategies and initiatives aimed at protecting the environment. Analyzing real-world case studies of successful conservation efforts, they develop critical evaluation skills, understanding the intricate factors contributing to their effectiveness. Simultaneously, their communication skills flourish as they incorporate discourse markers, enhancing the organization and progression of their ideas. Further refining their expression, students apply meticulous revision and editing techniques, ensuring the clarity and coherence of their written work. Through this integrated approach, students emerge not only with a profound understanding of environmental stewardship and conservation but also with polished communication and writing abilities, empowering them to articulate their ideas effectively and persuasively in various contexts.

What's the Big Idea?

Students will examine the importance of conservation and environmental stewardship.

Success Criteria

  • Utilize discourse markers effectively, employing words and phrases to signal organization, progression, and connections in both written and spoken discourse
  • Apply revision and editing techniques, to refine sentence structures, 
  • Critically analyze case studies of successful environmental conservation efforts
