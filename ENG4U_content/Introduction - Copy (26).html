Qualitative & Quantitative Sources 

Lesson Description

In this lesson students will look at how research is analyzed based on qualitative and quantitative methods. They will learn how different methods are used (ie. surveys, focus groups, interviews, literature reviews, etc.). At the end of the lesson students will have the opportunity to identify how their own research will be analyzed.

Overall Expectations

B1 Reading for Meaning: read and demonstrate an understanding of a variety of literary, informational, and graphic texts, using a range of strategies to construct meaning.

C1 Developing and Organizing Content: generate, gather, and organize ideas and information to write for an intended purpose and audience.

D1 Understanding Media Texts: demonstrate an understanding of a variety of media texts.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 read a variety of student- and teacher-selected texts from diverse cultures and historical periods, identifying specific purposes for reading

B1.2 select and use, with increasing facility, the most appropriate reading comprehension strategies to understand texts, including complex and challenging texts.

C1.3 Locate and select information to fully and effectively support ideas for writing, using a variety of strategies and print, electronic, and other resources, as appropriate.

C1.5 Determine whether the ideas and information gathered are accurate and complete, interesting, and effectively meet the requirements of the writing task.

D1.2 Interpret media texts, including complex or challenging texts, identifying and explaining with increasing insight the overt and implied messages they convey.

D1.3 evaluate how effectively information, ideas, themes, issues, and opinions are communicated in media texts, including complex and challenging texts, and decide whether the texts.

Learning Goals

  • Students will recall their knowledge of quantitative and qualitative research. 
  • Students will learn common methods used in quantitative and qualitative research.
  • Students will determine when to use quantitative and qualitative research methods. 
  • Students will discover how to interpret the data collected from quantitative and qualitative research methods. 
  • Students will apply what they have learned about quantitative and qualitative methods in their research. 

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

  • I can recall my previous knowledge of quantitative and qualitative research. 
  • I can identify common methods used in quantitative and qualitative research.
  • I can determine when to use quantitative and qualitative research methods. 
  • I can interpret the data collected from quantitative and qualitative research methods. 
  • I can apply what I learned about quantitative and qualitative methods in my research.
