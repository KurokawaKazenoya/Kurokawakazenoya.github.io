Literary Theories Run Through:  Society/Context-Based Theories

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will learn about the society/context based literary theories. You will learn how to distinguish them from each other, and you will also learn how to interpret a literary piece by the use of a literary theory. You will choose a theory and use it to analyze an independent novel study as an assignment.

Overall Expectations

  • B1 Reading for Meaning: read and demonstrate an understanding of a variety of literary, informational, and graphic texts, using a range of strategies to construct meaning.
  • B2 Understanding Form and Style: recognize a variety of text forms, text features, and stylistic elements and demonstrate an understanding of how they help communicate meaning.
  • C3 Applying Knowledge of Conventions: use editing, proofreading, and publishing skills and strategies, and knowledge of language conventions to correct errors, refine expression, and present their work effectively

Specific Expectations

  • B1.1 Read a variety of student- and teacher-selected texts from diverse cultures and historical periods, identifying specific purposes for reading.
  • B1.4Make and explain inferences of increasing subtlety and insight about texts, including complex and challenging texts, supporting their explanations with well-chosen stated and implied ideas from the texts.
  • B1.5 Extend understanding of texts, including complex and challenging texts, by making rich and increasingly insightful connections between the ideas in them and personal knowledge, experience, and insights; other texts; and the world around them.
  • B2.1 Identify a variety of characteristics of literary, informational, and graphic text forms and explain how they help communicate meaning.
  • B2.3 Identify a variety of elements of style in texts and explain how they help communicate meaning and enhance the effectiveness of the texts.
  • C3.4 Use grammar conventions correctly and appropriately to communicate their intended meaning clearly and effectively.

Learning Goals

  • Students will know the different literary theories
  • Students will distinguish between the literary theories
  • Students will interpret a literary piece with the use of literary theories

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

  • Identify the different literary theories
  • Know the difference between the literary theories
  • Write an interpretation of a story/novel of your choice based on a literary theory.
