Lesson 5

In this imaginative and linguistic-focused lesson, students delve into the intricacies of storytelling, examining the elements that create compelling narratives. Engaging in discussions, they dissect the components of compelling stories, appreciating the nuances of plot, character, and theme. Simultaneously, they identify themes challenging societal norms, honing their critical analysis skills by exploring narratives that contribute to social change. The creative realm opens as students craft their own short stories or excerpts from banned books, applying their understanding of storytelling techniques to create captivating narratives that challenge established norms. Linguistic proficiency is refined through the practice of nominalization, enhancing the precision and variety of their writing. Furthermore, students explore the art of epistolary writing, mastering the craft of letter composition and imbuing their characters or perspectives with depth and authenticity. Through this comprehensive approach, students not only refine their creative writing skills but also enhance their linguistic versatility, enabling them to express complex ideas and diverse voices with clarity and creativity.

What's the Big Idea?

Students will synthesize their understanding of the power of literature and storytelling by creating their own banned book.

Success Criteria

  • Create a short story or excerpt from a banned book

  • Convert verbs, adjectives, or other parts of speech into nouns for variety and precision in writing

  • Craft letters from different perspectives, characters, or time periods
