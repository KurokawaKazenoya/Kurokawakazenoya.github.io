Being: Wise one, here are the questions for you if you seek a biographical, psychological, or historical approach. Remember the words life, mind, and time.

Open All Panels

Biographical

  • What verifiable aspects of the author’s biography show up in his/her work?
  • Do places where the author grew up in appear in the text?
  • How does the author weave aspects of his/her familial life into the literary text world?  
  • What distinguishes the author from his or her persona in the text?

Psychological

  • How does the work reflect the writer’s psychology?
  • How does the work reflect the unconscious dimensions of the writer’s mind?

Historical

  • How does it reflect the time in which it was written?
  • How accurately does the story depict the time in which it is set in?
  • What literary or historical influences helped to shape the form and content of the work? 
  • What historical events or movements might have influenced this writer?
  • How important is it the historical context (the work’s and the reader’s) in interpreting the work?
