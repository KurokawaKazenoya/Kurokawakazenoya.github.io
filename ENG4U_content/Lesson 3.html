Lesson

Students explore the art of effective communication through parallel structure and precision in language. They learn to maintain consistency in sentence structure, emphasizing clarity and enhancing the impact of their ideas. Through engaging discussions, students delve into themes of self-discovery, acceptance, and the influence of societal expectations on identity. By focusing on precise word choice and eliminating ambiguity, students hone their language skills, conveying nuanced meanings within sentences and paragraphs. This lesson fosters critical thinking, empathy, and articulate expression, empowering students to navigate complex themes while enhancing their communication abilities.

What's the Big Idea?

Students will analyze literary works that delve into the concept of self-discovery, acceptance, and impact.

Contextualizing Grammar

Parallel Structure: Maintaining consistency in sentence structure for clarity and emphasis.

To understand parallel structure better, let's take a look at our reading for the lesson, the article titled "Being Filipino: Identity, Culture, and Belonging Among Winnipeg Teens" from CBC News. This article discusses the experiences of Filipino teens living in Winnipeg, Canada. While the article may not directly relate to parallel structure, we can still extract examples and quotes to illustrate its significance.

Example 1:

Original sentence from the article: "It's about preserving our culture, our traditions, but also embracing our Canadian identity as well."

 In this sentence, the author uses parallel structure to emphasize the importance of both preserving Filipino culture and embracing Canadian identity. By using the parallel structure "preserving our culture, our traditions" and "embracing our Canadian identity," the author effectively communicates the dual nature of the teens' experiences.

Example 2:

Original sentence from the article: "It's about finding a balance between the two, between being Filipino and being Canadian."

 Here, the author again employs parallel structure to highlight the need for balance between the teens' Filipino and Canadian identities. The use of the parallel structure "between the two" and "between being Filipino and being Canadian" reinforces the idea of finding equilibrium in their cultural identities.

By analyzing these examples from the article, we can see how parallel structure enhances the clarity and emphasis of the writer's message. When constructing sentences, it is crucial to ensure that similar ideas are presented in a parallel manner. This consistency not only aids comprehension but also adds a certain rhythm and impact to the writing.
