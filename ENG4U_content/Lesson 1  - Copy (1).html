Lesson 1

In this enlightening lesson, students embark on an exploration of banned books, delving into the complex nuances of literary censorship. They begin by defining the concept, and gaining a solid understanding of literature that has been prohibited or censored for various reasons. Engaging discussions follow, where students dissect the multifaceted reasons behind banning books, developing critical thinking skills as they consider the societal, cultural, and political factors at play. Through in-depth analysis, students explore the profound impact of banned books on individuals and societies, delving into psychological, cultural, and societal repercussions with empathy and insight. Concurrently, linguistic proficiency is honed as students master the usage of quantifiers, ensuring accurate expression in various contexts. Their literary analysis abilities flourish as they seamlessly integrate quotations into their writing, enhancing the depth and persuasiveness of their analyses. Through this multifaceted approach, students emerge with a nuanced understanding of banned books, refined language skills, and advanced analytical and persuasive writing abilities, empowering them to engage thoughtfully with complex literary and societal issues.

What's the Big Idea?

Students will analyze the impact of literature on individuals and societies by examining the concept of banned books.

Success Criteria

  • Integrate quotations seamlessly into sentences and paragraphs

  • Apply knowledge of quantifiers, understanding words like much, many, few, little, some, any, all, enough, etc., in various contexts

  • Critically analyze the impact of banned books on individuals and societies
