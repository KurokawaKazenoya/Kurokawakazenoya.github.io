Lesson 5

In this culminating lesson, students refine their literary critique skills, learning to craft well-structured paragraphs that incorporate critical perspectives and evaluative judgments, enriching their ability to analyze literature effectively. Concurrently, they master the identification and punctuation of appositives, enhancing the precision and clarity of their sentences. Through a comprehensive review, students solidify their knowledge of key concepts related to environmental sustainability. Engaging in reflective discussions, they share personal insights and engage in collaborative learning, enriching their understanding through diverse perspectives. Moreover, students are encouraged to exercise creative and critical thinking, exploring innovative solutions for environmental challenges and evaluating their practicality, fostering imaginative problem-solving skills. This multifaceted approach equips students with refined literary analysis abilities, grammatical proficiency, and a deepened understanding of environmental sustainability, empowering them to articulate their thoughts persuasively and think critically about ecological issues.

What's the Big Idea?

Students will synthesize their learning and reflect on humanity's relationship with the natural world.

Success Criteria

  • Structure paragraphs for literary critiques, seamlessly incorporating critical perspectives and evaluative judgments
  • Construct coherent and persuasive arguments that enhance the depth and insight of their literary analyses.
  • Identify appositives within sentences, punctuating noun phrases that rename or explain other nouns accurately
