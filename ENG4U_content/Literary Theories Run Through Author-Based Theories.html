Literary Theories Run Through: Author-Based Theories

Author-based theories

focus on the author and his influence on the literary piece.

Biographical Psychological

Focuses on the author’s life and experiences.

 

relies on a close examination of character and motivations and their symbolic meanings (impulses, desires, feelings) that might be linked with the author.

Example of a biographical approach in literature:


John Carey, the author of William Golding: The Man Who Wrote Lord of the Flies, wrote that Golding "was scared of being alone in the night," as were the littluns. This fear creates the idea of the beast, the driving force for the boys to become even more savage and uncivilized.  

Example of a biographical approach in literature:

World War II influenced the themes and setting of Lord of the Flies. This could be because, during World War II, Golding fought battleships at the sinking of the Bismarck and also fended off submarines and planes. 

Examines how the author’s emotional upbringing or psychological history impacts a text.

Example of a psychological approach in the story Lord of the Flies by William Golding: 

While growing up, Golding often mocked his father's view of rationalism, believing that our world can be understood through reasoning. The character Piggy exemplifies this belief, while all other characters, at one point or another, become his foil. When the hunters murder the level-headed Piggy, Golding proves that basic human nature opposes the ideals of rationalism.

Example of a psychological approach in the story Lord of the Flies by William Golding:

In his biographies, he admits to being a brat as a child and  quoted, "I enjoyed hurting people." Later on in his World War II experiences, Golding has said, “I began to see what people were capable of doing. Anyone who moved through those years without understanding that man produces evil as a bee produces honey, must have been blind or wrong in the head.” Like his teaching experience, Golding’s participation in the war would prove to be fruitful material for his fiction.
