Step 1: Source/Sender

The source is where the communication starts.&nbsp;
Whatever background the source has determines the type of message sent.

Step 2: Message

This is what the source intends to send.

Step 3: Encoding

The source sends the information through encoding.&nbsp;
This also determines what means the communication goes through.

Step 4: Channel

This is the medium or media the message goes through.

Step 5: Decoding

The process where the receiver begins to interpret the message is called decoding. 
The manner in which the receiver gets the message affects how he/she views the message.

Step 6: Receiver

The receiver is the intended person to receive the message. 
Their background also determines how they will interpret the message.

Step 7: Feedback

This is the response of the receiver. 
When the receiver responds to the message, the communication process becomes dynamic.
