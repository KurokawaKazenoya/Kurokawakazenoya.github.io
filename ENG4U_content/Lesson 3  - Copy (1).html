Lesson 3

Engaging in critical discussions, students explore the pivotal role of storytelling in challenging established norms,and understanding the power of narratives to provoke thought and drive social change. Simultaneously, linguistic skills are refined as students comprehend the inversion of word order for stylistic and emphatic purposes, recognizing its application in literature for expressive communication. Their analytical prowess further advances as they craft thematic paragraphs, delving deep into specific themes within literary works, and constructing arguments rich in evidence and insight. Through this comprehensive approach, students emerge with a heightened understanding of the subversive potential of literature, polished language proficiency, and advanced analytical abilities, empowering them to engage critically, persuasively, and empathetically with societal norms and literary narratives.

What's the Big Idea?

Students will examine how stories shape perceptions and challenge norms by analyzing specific examples from banned books.

Success Criteria
