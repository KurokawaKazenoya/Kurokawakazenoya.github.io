Transition Words and Phrases

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will look at the various methods in which transition words and phrases can be effective in writing. They will practice implementing various phrases into their own sentences. The main application task will be to take the learned material and apply it to a short narrative essay.

Overall Expectations

B2 Understanding Form and Style: recognize a variety of text forms, text features, and stylistic elements and demonstrate understanding of how they help communicate meaning.
C1 Developing and Organizing Content: generate, gather, and organize ideas and information to write for an intended purpose and audience.
C2 . Using Knowledge of Form and Style: draft and revise their writing, using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms and stylistic elements appropriate for the purpose and audience.
C3 Applying Knowledge of Conventions: use editing, proofreading, and publishing skills and strategies, and knowledge of language conventions, to correct errors, refine expression, and present their work effectively.

Specific Expectations

B2.1 Identify a variety of characteristics of literary, informational, and graphic text forms and demonstrate insight into the way they help communicate meaning 
C1.4 Identify, sort, and order main ideas and supporting details for writing tasks, using a variety of strategies and selecting the organizational pattern best suited to the content and the purpose for writing.
C2.1 Write for different purposes and audiences using a variety of literary, informational, and graphic forms.
C3.1 Use knowledge of spelling rules and patterns, a variety of resources, and appropriate strategies to recognize and correct their own and others’ spelling errors.
C3.3 Use punctuation correctly and effectively to communicate their intended meaning.
C3.4 Use grammar conventions correctly and appropriately to communicate their intended meaning clearly and effectively.

Learning Goals

  • Students will recognize the different types of transition words and phrases used in writing.
  • Students will demonstrate and communicate their understanding of the different transition words and phrases by writing their own examples.

Success Criteria

You will know you are successful in this lesson if you:

  • I can recognize the different types of transition words and phrases used in writing.
  • I can demonstrate and communicate my understanding of the different transition words and phrases by writing my own examples.
