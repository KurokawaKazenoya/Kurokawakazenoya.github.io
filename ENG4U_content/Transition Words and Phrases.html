Effective Communication 

  

Recipes contain the exact ingredients and step-by-step procedure for cooking the meal you want.

When we see things in order, we understand it quickly.

Aside from putting numbers, we can also use words such as first, second, third, lastly, etc. to indicate the order of the steps in a procedure.

First, second, this and lastly are some examples of transition words.

Transitions in Writing
  • In writing, transition words and phrases are used to create a smooth flow between thoughts.
  • Using sophisticated transitional devices will ensure that your essay moves smoothly from one paragraph to another within the essay, and from one point to another within the body paragraphs.

Accordion

Transitions: For Adding Ideas

Examples of Transitional Words/Phrases:

  • however, moreover; and besides; then; equally important; not only...but also; also; likewise; further; furthermore; in addition; again (do not use firstly, secondly, thirdly, and so on)

Example of Use:

  • Garlic has always added flavor to homemade dishes. Furthermore, many studies show that it has plenty of medicinal properties.

Transitions: To Indicate Order

Examples of Transitional Words/Phrases:

  • to begin with; first of all; first; second; next; third; to conclude; finally; lastly; subsequently; consequently; concurrently; at the same time; previously; preceding this; simultaneously

Example of Use:

  • Writing a book is no easy task. First, you have to find an idea that interests you, and that task is challenging.

Transitions: For Opposing or Contrasting Ideas

Examples of Transitional Words/Phrases:

  • but; yet; actually; nor; although; however; still; nevertheless; on the other hand; on the contrary; by contrast; after all; differing from; neither...nor; notwithstanding; for all that; at the same time; even though; otherwise; in spite of; whereas; nonetheless

Example of Use:

  • Alexa is a diligent worker, but she is not receptive to criticism.

Transitions: For Comparing Ideas

Examples of Transitional Words/Phrases:

  • similarly, likewise; n like manner; by comparison; not only...but also; both...and either...or

Example of Use:

  • Choose one of the following options that applies to you: either keep writing every day or wait until inspiration strikes before you write.

Transitions: To Show Results or Consequences

Examples of Transitional Words/Phrases:

  • Then, afterwards; as a result; thereafter; finally; now; because; hence, consequently; so; therefore, accordingly; thus; thereupon

Example of Use:

  • She booked a flight to Vancouver this spring because she is planning to surprise her mom for her birthday.

Transitions: To Illustrate Ideas

Examples of Transitional Words/Phrases:

  • for example; for instance; thus; when; where; in this case; evidence of this; in this situation

Example of Use:

  • Nick got upset when his cousin commented on the shirt he bought on sale.

Transitions: To Intensify Ideas

Examples of Transitional Words/Phrases:

  • indeed, in truth, basically, of course, truly, certainly, essentially, definitely, undoubtedly, naturally.

Example of Use:

  • Max and Sam have been best friends since grade school. Naturally, Max asked him to be his best man for his wedding.

Transitions: To Summarize, Repeat, and Exemplify

Examples of Transitional Words/Phrases:

  • to sum up, in brief, on the whole, in short, in other words, that is, for example, for instance, in any event, in particular, once again, hence, in short, in conclusion, for this reason.

Example of Use:

  • You cannot simply demand respect; in other words, you have to earn it.

Transitions: To Indicate Time

Examples of Transitional Words/Phrases:

  • then, now, finally, meanwhile, eventually, next, hereafter, afterwards, immediately, soon, after, later, at last, as soon as, currently, presently, henceforth, previously.

Example of Use:

  • As soon as the dragon woke, Shrek and Donkey rushed Fiona out of the castle.

Transitions: To Indicate Place

Examples of Transitional Words/Phrases:

  • away from, beyond, here, nearby, opposite to, close to, adjacent to, on the opposite side, next to, on the left, across, among, below, beneath, besides, farther, there, under, over.

Example of Use:

  • Among her three siblings, Jim is the most intelligent.
