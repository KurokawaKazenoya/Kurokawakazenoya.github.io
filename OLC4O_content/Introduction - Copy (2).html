Lesson 1- Reading Strategies: Informational Texts

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will focus on reading informational texts and news reports. They will learn strategies to apply before, during, and after reading informational texts to improve their reading comprehension.

This lesson also touches on common structures of the informational text: description, sequence of events, cause and effect, and compare and contrast.

Overall Expectations

A1 Demonstrate the ability to read and respond to a variety of texts.

A2 Demonstrate understanding of the organizational structure and features of a variety of informational, narrative, and graphic texts, including information paragraphs, opinion pieces, textbooks, newspaper reports magazine stories, and short fiction.

A3 Demonstrate understanding of the content and meaning of informational, narrative, and graphic texts that they have read using a variety of reading strategies.

Specific Expectations

A1.1 Demonstrate that they have read a variety of student-selected and teacher-selected texts, including informational, narrative, and graphic texts.

A1.2 Demonstrate the ability to read independently for personal, school-related, and career-related purposes.

A1.3 Describe, orally and/or in writing, personally relevant texts they have read and their responses to them.

A2.2 Use knowledge of the organizational structure (e.g., layout, grid pattern) of a variety of graphic texts.

A3.2 Use appropriate pre-reading strategies to preview new texts.

A3.6 Use appropriate strategies to locate information in different types of texts.

A3.7 Use appropriate strategies to make inferences about and interpret different types of texts.

A3.8 Make connections between personal experiences and the content of texts to consolidate and extend understanding of different types of texts.

  

Learning Goals

  • Students will recognize and give samples of informational texts.

  • Students will know what informational texts will be included in the OSSLT.

  • Students will learn strategies before, during, and after reading informational texts.

  • Students will apply the strategies they learned in answering questions about a sample informational text.

    Success Criteria

    You know you are successful in the lesson if you can:

    • Provide accurate examples of informational texts.

    • Remember what informational texts are included in the OSSLT.

    • Apply the strategies before, during, and after reading informational texts.

    • Answer sample questions about the informational text with accuracy.
