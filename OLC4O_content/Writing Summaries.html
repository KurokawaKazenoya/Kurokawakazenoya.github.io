Writing Summaries

What is summary?

A summary is a short restatement of the main idea and important details in a piece of writing or another work.

Why do we summarize text?

Summarizing helps you:

  • Discern the most important ideas in a text.
  • Ignore irrelevant information, and
  • Integrate the central ideas in a meaningful way.
  • Improve memory for what is read.

How to summarize text

Accordion

Select each item to learn more.

Identify the topic and main idea of the text.

What is it trying to say? You can look at the title for clues.

Write down the most important ideas.

The details that relate to the main idea of the text on a separate piece of paper.

Use a highlighter to select important ideas that relate to the main ideas of the text.

Add these ideas to your rough work page.

Delete unnecessary information.

Read the text again and ask yourself "Does my information connect to the main idea of the text?"

There are two types of unnecessary information to delete:

The unimportant or trivial information - minor details about topics

The redundant information - includes rewording or restating some of the important sentences.

 

Write a beginning sentence that includes the main idea of the text.

 

From your notes page, select one supporting detail and write a sentence about it. Then write one more sentence that further explains or offers an example of that detail.

 

Repeat this step 2+ times to make the body of your summary.

Lastly, write a concluding sentence that sums up your summary paragraph. 

 

It should link to the beginning sentence of your summary (and the main idea of the text).

Writing summaries combines both the skill of reading and writing.

 

Follow the right process and you may communicate the message in a quick and effective manner.
