Articles

    

An, a, and the

Articles

  • Clarifies whether a noun is specific or general, singular or plural.
  • Appears before the noun.
  • Two types: definite and indefinite.

      

Definite article: the Indefinite article: a/an Special rules for articles General rules
  • To identify specific nouns.
  • Represents things, places, ideas, or persons that can be identified specifically.
  • Used for both singular and plural nouns.

Examples

  • The house (not just any house).
  • The students in Professor Smith’s class should study harder.
  • The story of us looks a lot like a tragedy now.
  • Identify nouns that are not definite and not specific.
  • Only for singular nouns.

When to use a and an

  • Use a before consonant sounds.
  • Use an before vowel sounds (except a long u), e.g. asset, index, apple, igloo.
  • Use a before a noun with a sounded h, a long u, and o with the sound of w (e.g. uniform, one-hour).
  • Use an when h is not sounded (e.g. honour, hour).
  • Place the article before the noun.
  • Article > Adjective > Noun is the proper format.
  • Do not add an article when the noun has a possessive pronoun (my, his, her, our, their).
  • Do not add an article when the noun has a demonstrative pronoun (this, that).
