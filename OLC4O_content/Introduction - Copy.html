Lesson 1 - Introduction to Literacy, Reading and Writing

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will learn about literacy as an ability, reflect on their current reading habits and understand the different applications of reading that can take place in other media, know how to use visual cues, contextual cues, and prediction to improve their reading skills, learn how to write critically by following the five stages of the writing process, get tips on how to be productive in each stage and reflect on other challenges they face when completing a writing task.

Overall Expectations

A1. Demonstrate the ability to read and respond to a variety of texts.

A2. Demonstrate understanding of the organizational structure and features of a variety of informational, narrative, and graphic texts, including information paragraphs, opinion pieces, textbooks, newspaper reports magazine stories, and short fiction.

A3. Demonstrate understanding of the content and meaning of informational, narrative, and graphic texts that they have read using a variety of reading strategies.

B1. Demonstrate the ability to use the writing process by generating and organizing ideas and producing first drafts, revised drafts, and final polished pieces to complete a variety of writing tasks.

C1. Demonstrate understanding of the importance of communication skills in their everyday lives – at school, at work, and home.

Specific Expectations

A1.1 Demonstrate that they have read a variety of student-selected and teacher-selected texts, including informational, narrative, and graphic texts.

A1.2 Demonstrate the ability to read independently for personal, school-related, and career-related purposes describe, orally and/or in writing, personally relevant texts they have read and their responses to them.

A2.1 Use knowledge of the organizational structure (e.g., layout, grid pattern) of a variety of graphic texts.

A3.2 Use appropriate pre-reading strategies to preview new texts.

A3.6 Use appropriate strategies to locate information in different types of texts.

A3.7 Use appropriate strategies to make inferences about and interpret different types of texts.

A3.8 Make connections between personal experiences and the content of texts to consolidate and extend understanding of different types of texts.

B1.2 Use prewriting strategies to generate ideas for writing.

B1.5 Use appropriate strategies to organize ideas and information for writing.

B1.6 Create a first draft that includes the main and supporting ideas in the required form.

B1.7 Revise drafts to ensure that ideas are presented in a logical order, discard irrelevant ideas and information, add details where information is insufficient, and ensure a tone and level of language appropriate to the audience and purpose, using appropriate strategies.

B1.9 Use appropriate strategies to edit written work.

C1.2 Explain how the ability to read, write, listen, and speak effectively can help them succeed at school, at work, and in their personal lives.

C1.3 Demonstrate understanding of the role of reading and writing in the learning process.

  

Learning Goals

  • Students will explain how the ability to read, write, listen, and speak effectively can help them succeed at school, at work, and in their personal lives.
  • Students will demonstrate an understanding of the role of reading and writing in the learning process.
  • Students will demonstrate that they have read a variety of student-selected and teacher-selected texts, including informational, narrative, and graphic texts.
  • Students will demonstrate the ability to read independently for personal, school-related, and career-related purposes, describe, orally and/or in writing, personally relevant texts they have read and their responses to them.
  • Students will get an overview of the reading process.
  • Students will learn the writing process.
  • Students will understand the importance of the writing process.
  • Students will practice writing an essay using the writing process.

    Success Criteria

    You know you are successful in the lesson if you can:

    • explain how the ability to read, write, listen, and speak effectively can help them succeed at school, at work, and in their personal lives.
    • demonstrate understanding of the role of reading and writing in the learning process.
    • get to know yourself as a reader.
    • understand how the reading process works.
    • remember how form helps in interpreting what you are reading.
    • identify the steps involved in the writing process.
    • understand the importance of the writing process for success in writing.
