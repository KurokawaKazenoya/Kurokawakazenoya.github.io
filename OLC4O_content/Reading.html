Reading

We often do not consider ourselves readers unless we have read many novels. 

We might not realize it, but reading takes place all the time. 

Text messages, song lyrics, manuals, instructions, street signs, even recipes, we have read them. 

 

Most people read a fair bit, but not everyone reads the same type of material. 

 

How about you? Which material do you read most often? 

  • Novels
  • News articles
  • Emails
  • Comics/Cartoons
  • Text Messages/Online Chat

You will find that it is easier to interpret information when presented in a familiar form. 

 

For instance, your strategies to understand text messages will be different from your approach to make sense of a novel you are reading.

The process of reading is the combination of all the exercises that you just went through. 



First, we pay attention to the form. This will help us identify the purpose of the reading material. 

Look at this image. What kind of reading material is this?

 


What is the purpose of the movie poster? 

Next, we look for the overall idea.

What do you think the movie is about?

Then, we examine smaller clues to predict what’s going to happen next. 


What details did you notice in the poster that can help you predict what will happen in the movie? 


 


Lastly, we think about how it relates to us.

After reading the poster, what questions do you have about the movie? 




We use the form to make assumptions, predictions and connect with what we are reading. 

 

Quite simply, paying attention to the form will help you read more successfully.
