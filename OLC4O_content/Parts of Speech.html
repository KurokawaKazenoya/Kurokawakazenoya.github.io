Parts of Speech

 

In the previous lesson, you learned about literacy, the reading and writing process.

Now, let’s proceed to learn about the different parts of speech.

      

There are eight parts of speech: noun, pronoun, verb, adjective, adverb, preposition, conjunction, and interjection.

They indicate how a word functions grammatically and in definition within the sentence.

          

Now let’s take a closer look at the eight parts of speech:

Noun

A noun is the name of a person, a place, a thing, or an idea. (e.g. Human, Kanata Academy, etc.)

  • Proper nouns always start with a capital letter, while common nouns don’t.

Pronoun

A pronoun is a word used in place of a noun. (e.g. he, she, it, etc.)

  • The pronoun is often substituted with its antecedent. For example, the word “man” can be substituted with “he.”

Adjective

An adjective describes or modifies a noun or a pronoun. (e.g. new, yellow, cute, etc.)

  • It answers the question of what kind, how many, or which one.

Adverb

An adverb describes or modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb. (e.g. carefully, slowly, etc.)

It answers the question of why, when, where, or how.

  • Tip: adverbs often end in -ly

Preposition

To indicate direction, time, place, location, or spatial relationships, or to introduce an object, a preposition is a word or set of words used before a noun, pronoun, or noun phrase.

Conjunction

You use conjunctions to join words, phrases, or clauses and indicate the relationship between both elements.

  • Coordinating conjunctions join grammatically equal elements, subordinating conjunctions connect unequal clauses, and correlative conjunctions use pairs to join words or phrases of equal importance.

Interjection

  • An interjection is used to express emotion. (e.g. wow! Oh! etc.)

Verb

  • Verbs are words that show an action (sing), occurrence (develop), or state of being (exist)
