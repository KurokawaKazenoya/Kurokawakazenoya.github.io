Diction                       

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will identify diction and why it matters when it comes to improving their literacy skills. You will learn formal, informal and colloquial diction and when to use each one appropriately. They will also discuss denotation versus connotation. Finally, you will watch the video, Is There an Alternative to Political Correctness? to learn about political correctness in relation to diction.

Overall Expectations

A1  Demonstrate the ability to read and respond to a variety of texts.

A3 Demonstrate understanding of the content and meaning of informational, narrative, and graphic texts that they have read using a variety of reading strategies.

A4 Use a variety of strategies to understand unfamiliar and specialized words and expressions in informational, narrative, and graphic texts.

B1 Demonstrate the ability to use the writing process by generating and organizing ideas and producing first drafts, revised drafts, and final polished pieces to complete a variety of writing tasks.

C1 Demonstrate understanding of the importance of communication skills in their everyday lives – at school, at work, and at home.

Specific Expectations

A1.3 Describe, orally and/or in writing, personally relevant texts they have read and their responses to them.

A3.4 Use appropriate strategies to monitor comprehension when reading informational and narrative texts.

A3.7 Use appropriate strategies to make inferences about and interpret different types of texts.

A4.1 Use appropriate strategies to discover the meaning of unfamiliar and technical words encountered in their reading.

B1.3 Use oral language skills, in English or a first language, to develop content for writing.

C1.1 Describe the communication skills they need to function effectively in a variety of situations at school, at work and in daily life.

Success Criteria

When writing learning objectives for a course or module, remember:

  • Define key terms related to diction.
  • Connect how diction affects oral literacy.
  • Learn the four types of diction. Differentiate connotation from denotation.
  • Watch a video to learn articulation exercises.

Learning Goals

  • Students will define key terms related to diction.
  • Students will realize how diction helps in oral literacy.
  • Students will learn the four types of diction.
  • Students will differentiate connotative from denotative meaning.
  • Students will watch a video to learn articulation exercises.
