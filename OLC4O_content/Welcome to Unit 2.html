Welcome to Unit 2

Unit 2 is dedicated to refining advanced reading and writing skills, offering a comprehensive approach to literacy. In the first segment, students delve into reading informational texts and news reports, acquiring strategies to bolster comprehension throughout the reading process. The lesson also delves into the structural intricacies of informational texts, encompassing description, sequence of events, cause and effect, and compare and contrast.

The subsequent focus shifts to information writing in the following segment. Students not only define information writing but also recognize its importance. The lesson guides them through the five stages of the writing process—prewriting, drafting, revising, proofreading, and final draft. Practical application involves crafting organized informational texts about notable figures, with a focus on Bob Marley, Adele, and Drake. Clear and concise writing strategies are emphasized, providing students with the tools to articulate information effectively.

The unit concludes by introducing graphic texts and elucidating their purpose. Students familiarize themselves with samples of graphic texts and learn strategies to enhance their engagement before, during, and after reading. Collectively, these lessons equip students with advanced literacy tools, spanning strategic reading, organized writing, and the interpretation of graphic texts.
