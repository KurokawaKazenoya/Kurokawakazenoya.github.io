Instructions:



Overview

Oral literacy is a great tool in expressing your knowledge, ideas, and feelings. Do you have a recent idea worth sharing? 

Your task is to create a mock TEDx video about an idea that you would like to share. Use your knowledge of vocabulary, diction, and oral fluency in making this video presentation. 

What is TEDx? 

TEDx Talks are a showcase for speakers presenting well-informed ideas under 18 minutes. To learn more, watch this video. 

Where do I find examples of TEDx talks? 

You can find the library of TEDx talks on either their: 

  • YouTube channel 

  • TEDx website 

Watch some TEDx videos to get familiar with the form. 

How do I create a TEDx talk for this assessment? 

Here are some simple steps to get you started. 

Step 1: Develop an idea


Think of a new or surprising topic. It could also be something that challenges an existing belief. The TED website has a list of topics that can help you get inspired. Visit the website. 

Step 2: Make an outline or script


Every talk follows a basic formula: 

  • Introduction- could be a question, a statement that will make your audience think. A strong introduction is crucial. 

  • Body- this is where you make a list of evidence that supports your topic. 

  • Conclusion- this is your call for action. Something that will make your audience feel positive about what you just presented. 


You can always ask for feedback from your teacher as soon as you're done with your first draft. 

Step 3: Rehearse


Practice reading your script. Rehearse! Rehearse! Rehearse!

Step 4: Give your talk 


Create a video of your written and rehearsed TEDx Talk. It is not a requirement, but feel free to use any editing software to enhance your video. 

Reflect

Once you are done creating your video, answer the following questions in your copy of this document. 

  • Why did you choose to talk about the topic in your video? 

  • What literacy skills did you apply to complete your video? 

  • How do you plan in communicating your idea to your friends, classmates, family or community? 

Teacher Notes 

Use the rubric provided by your teacher as a guide in finalizing your video. Upload a copy of your TEDx video and this document to your shared course folder. Notify your teacher once you are finished.
