Hangul


The Korean language is an invented language.

Korean originated in Chinese.

The Korean alphabet is called Hangul.

Hangul was created by King Sejong in 1443.

Hangul was released to the public in 1446.

King Sejong was a linguist. He and a group of linguists created Hangul.

Hangul is phonetic.

King Sejong said: “A genius can learn it in an hour, but even a fool could learn it in a day.”

In the 15th century, only nobles were literate. This is why King Sejong created Hangul.

Hangul allowed working class citizens to be able to read and write.

Hangul was easier to understand and produce than traditional Chinese characters.
