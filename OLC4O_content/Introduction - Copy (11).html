Vocabulary

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will recognize how oral literacy helps in reading and writing effectively. You will learn about vocabulary and how to expand their vocabulary by determining meaning from context, identifying prefixes and suffixes and how they change or add meaning to words and using antonyms and synonyms to enforce logical relationships between word meanings. You will also practice exercises to improve their word library. Complete this lesson by answering the classwork and writing in your learning journal. 

Overall Expectations

A3 Demonstrate understanding of the content and meaning of informational, narrative, and graphic texts that they have read using a variety of reading strategies.
A4 Use a variety of strategies to understand unfamiliar and specialized words and expressions in informational, narrative, and graphic texts.
B1 Demonstrate the ability to use the writing process by generating and organizing ideas and producing first drafts, revised drafts, and final polished pieces to complete a variety of writing tasks.
C1 Demonstrate understanding of the importance of communication skills in their everyday lives – at school, at work, and at home.
C3 Demonstrate understanding of the reading and writing processes and of the role of reading and writing in learning.

Specific Expectations

A 3.8 Make connections between personal experiences and the content of texts to consolidate and extend understanding of different types of texts.
A4.1 Use appropriate strategies to discover the meaning of unfamiliar and technical words encountered in their reading.
A4.3 Use appropriate strategies to expand their vocabulary through reading.
B1.3 Use oral language skills, in English or a first language, to support and enhance their reading experiences.
C1.2 Explain how the ability to read, write, listen, and speak effectively can help them to succeed at school, at work, and in their personal lives.
C3.2 Describe the communication skills they need to function effectively in a variety of situations at school, at work and in daily life.

Success Criteria

You are successful in this lesson if you can:

  • Recognize how oral literacy helps in reading and writing effectively.
  • Learn three techniques to improve your vocabulary.
  • Apply the techniques you learned by answering a vocabulary quiz with accuracy.

Learning Goals

  • Students will recognize the importance of oral literacy in reading and writing.
  • Students will learn three techniques to improve their vocabulary.
  • Students will take a vocabulary assessment to apply the techniques they learned.
