Transition Words and Phrases

   

Imagine two islands. You want to get to the other side, but there is no bridge. So, you build one. Now you can cross over to the other side.

That’s how transition words work, they “bridge” ideas together.

In writing, transition words and phrases are used to create a smooth flow between thoughts.

  • Using sophisticated transitional devices will ensure that your essay moves smoothly from one paragraph to another within the essay and from one point to another within the body paragraphs.

    

There are different types of Transition Words and Phrases

  

Accordion

For adding ideas

However, moreover, and, besides, then, equally important, not only...but also, also, likewise, further, furthermore, in addition, again (do not use firstly, secondly, thirdly, and so on).

  • Example: Jonathan is a great teacher; moreover, he is a friendly one.

To indicate order

To begin with, first of all, first, second, next, third, to conclude, finally, lastly, subsequently, consequently, concurrently, at the same time, previously, preceding this, simultaneously, concurrently.

  • Example: ...Lastly, it is important to wash your hands before eating.

For opposing or contrasting ideas

But, yet, actually, nor, although, however, still, nevertheless, on the other hand, on the contrary, by contrast, after all, differing from, neither...nor, notwithstanding, for all that, at the same time, even though, otherwise, in spite of, whereas nonetheless.

  • Example: John worked all day; nevertheless, he couldn’t finish all his tasks.

For comparing ideas

Similarly, likewise, in like manner, by comparison, not only...but also, both...and, either...or.

  • Example: By comparison, iPhones are more secure than Android phones

To show results or consequences

Then, afterwards, as a result, hereafter, finally, now, because, hence, consequently, so, therefore, accordingly, thus, thereupon.

  • Example: It rained during Maroon 5’s concert; as a result, the event was cancelled.

To illustrate ideas

For example, for instance, thus, when, where, in this case, evidence of this, in this situation.

  • Example: Alex has anger issues; for example, he got angry because I forgot to greet him good morning.

To intensify ideas

Indeed, in truth, basically, of course, truly, certainly, essentially, definitely, undoubtedly, naturally.

  • Example: Mabelle studied the whole day for the exam; naturally, it was the right thing to do.

To summarize, repeat, and exemplify

To sum up, in brief, on the whole, in short, in other words, that is, for example, for instance, in any event, in particular, once again, hence, in short, in conclusion, for this reason.

  • Example: In conclusion, my name is Inigo Montoya, and I am a pirate.

To indicate time

Then, now, finally, meanwhile, eventually, next, hereafter, afterwards, immediately, soon, after, later, at last, as soon as, currently, presently, henceforth, previously.

  • Example: At last, I have finally found my hidden treasure!

To indicate place

Away from, beyond, here, nearby, opposite to, close to, adjacent to, on the opposite side, next to, on the left, across, among, below, beneath, beside, farther, there, under, over.

  • Example: Beneath the quarry, there is a hidden stash of money.
