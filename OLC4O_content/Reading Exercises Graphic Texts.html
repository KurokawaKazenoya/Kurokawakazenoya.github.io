Reading Exercises: Graphic Texts

   

What is graphic text?

Graphic text presents ideas and information with the help of visual features.

These graphic features may be diagrams, photographs, drawings, sketches, patterns, timetables, maps, charts, or tables.

         

 

  

Why do people read or write Graphic Text?

People read graphic texts when they want to find out information about a specific topic.

  • The graphics make the information easier to understand.

During Reading

After your pre-reading activities, here are some strategies that you can apply while reading the graphic text.

    

Accordion

Select each item to learn more.

Tip #1

Read all the labels and examine how they are related to the graphic.

Each label has a purpose. The most essential labels may be in capital letters, bold type, or larger font.

Tip #2

Follow the arrows and lines. They may be used to show movement or direction or connect to the things they name.

Tip #3

Look for the use of color or symbols to emphasize important words and information. Some graphical texts have a legend or a key to explain the meaning of specific symbols and colors.

Tip #4

Study the image carefully. See if you recognize the details in the image. Read the text near the picture to find an explanation of the information in the graphic. Use the figure number or title and keywords to find and read the related information in the written text.

Tip #5

Identify the relationships between the visuals and information presented.
