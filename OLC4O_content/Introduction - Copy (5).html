Lesson 1 - Pronoun-Antecedent Agreement, Articles

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will learn how to identify the antecedent of the pronoun. You will also understand the different personal pronouns and how to use the correct pronoun based on the rules of pronoun-antecedent agreement, learn about the articles, and learn to use appropriate strategies to edit written work. To finish this lesson, you will have to continue working on your journal.

Overall Expectations

B1. Demonstrate the ability to use the writing process by generating and organizing ideas and producing first drafts, revised drafts, and final polished pieces to complete a variety of writing tasks.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 Identify the topic, the audience, the purpose for writing, and the requirements of the particular writing form.

B1.4 Use appropriate strategies to organize ideas and information for writing.

B1.9 use appropriate strategies to edit written work.

  

Learning Goals

  • Students will learn about the pronoun-antecedent agreement and its rules.

  • Students will apply pronoun-antecedent agreement in the quiz and in their writing.

  • Students will use appropriate strategies to edit written work to achieve accuracy in the use of the conventions of standard Canadian English, including the requirements of grammar, usage, spelling, and punctuation listed below: write complete and correct sentences.

    Success Criteria

    You know you are successful in the lesson if you can:

    • Learn about the pronoun-antecedent agreement and its rules.

    • Apply the pronoun-antecedent agreement in the quiz and in their writing.

    • Write complete and correct sentences.

    • Differentiate definite and indefinite articles.

    • Use definite articles appropriately.

    • Use indefinite articles appropriately.
