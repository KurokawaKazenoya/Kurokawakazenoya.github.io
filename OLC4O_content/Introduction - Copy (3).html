Lesson 2 - Writing Strategies: Informational Texts

Lesson Description

In this lesson, students will define information writing and its importance. Students will review the five stages of the writing process: prewriting, drafting, revising, proofreading, final draft and practice applying it to write organized informational texts on Bob Marley, Adele and Drake using information supplied by the teacher. You will also get strategies for clear and concise writing.

Overall Expectations

B1 Demonstrate the ability to use the writing process by generating and organizing ideas and producing first drafts, revised drafts, and final polished pieces to complete a variety of writing tasks.

B2 Use knowledge of writing forms and of the connections between form, audience, and purpose, to write summaries, information paragraphs, opinion pieces.

Specific Expectations

B1.2 Use prewriting strategies to generate ideas for writing.

B1.5 Use appropriate strategies to organize ideas and information for writing.

B1.6 Create a first draft that includes the main and supporting ideas in the required form.

B1.7 Revise drafts to ensure that ideas are presented in a logical order, to discard irrelevant ideas and information, to add details Where information is insufficient, and to ensure a tone and level of language appropriate to the audience and purpose, using appropriate strategies.

B1.9 Use appropriate strategies to edit written work.

B2.1 Explain the purpose and uses of information paragraphs.

B2.2 Construct clear, complete information paragraphs, some with graphic elements, for a variety of purposes, using correct Paragraph structure.

  

Learning Goals

  • Students will learn what information writing is.

  • Students will understand the importance and application of information writing.

  • Students will learn different approaches to information writing.

  • Students will learn strategies they can use in writing the information paragraph.

  • Students will write an informative text.

    Success Criteria

    You know you are successful in the lesson if you can:

    • Understand what is information writing and its importance.

    • Understand strategies they can use in information writing.

    • Write an informational text.
