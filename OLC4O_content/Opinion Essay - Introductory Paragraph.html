Opinion Essay - Introductory Paragraph

 

You will always need to provide an opinion about something and support your opinion with proof.

For example, in a job interview, you will need to prove that you deserve that job; once you have a job, you may need to prove that you deserve a promotion/raise. 

So how do we write opinion essays?

Newspapers also have opinion pieces or editorials written by editors to discuss a specific topic or issue.

How to write an opinion piece

The first thing to remember is to follow the Writing Process as a guide to help you finish the piece in no time:

  • Prewriting
  • Drafting
  • Revising
  • Proofreading
  • Final Draft

Step #2

Choose a side and draft a starting sentence that states your opinion.

  • Make sure there are at least three reasons that support your position. 
  • Each of your reasons should have an explanation that further explains your perspective.
  • You can provide a number of good examples, situations, and/or facts to support your idea.

Step #3

Write your draft. Opinion pieces typically comprise of the following sections:

  • An engaging introduction with a reasonable, specific opinion that can be supported by evidence.
  • A series of paragraphs with a varied range of evidence from credible sources that support the opinion.
  • A conclusion that will adequately summarize the essay and reinforce the opinion.

Let’s look at how to build each paragraph.

The Introductory Paragraph introduces the topic in general. It contains these parts:

First sentence 

The Hook

Second sentence Thesis 

Third to fifth sentence Arguments 

Sixth sentence Wrap up.

It should grab the reader’s interest right away and encourage him/her to continue reading. 



It’s helpful to start with a statistic or fact about your topic.

This sentence tells the reader what your argument is. 





It is your opinion, but you cannot use personal pronouns, such as “my” or “I.” 

Give an overview of the reasons which support your opinion.

This sentence wraps up your paragraph by summarizing your thesis/opinion.

Example:

  • There are many electronic devices that an individual needs to exist in society. Particularly, teenagers need to have cell-phones. Teenagers need to have cell-phones because it helps them to stay safe. Teenagers also need to have cell-phones to remain connected to their friends. Finally, cell-phones have many applications on them that can be used to help students at school. Indeed, all teenagers should have a cell-phone.
