Vocabulary

Whether you chose to relay your messages in writing or by allowing the other person to hear your voice, we all need the ability to communicate our thoughts effectively.

It is also important to note that you need to learn how to speak first before reading and writing. Oral language provides the foundation for literacy development, and one way of achieving oral literacy is through improving your vocabulary. 

WHAT IS VOCABULARY?

Vocabulary is the knowledge of words and their meanings. 

EXPANDING YOUR VOCABULARY

There are numerous ways to improve your vocabulary. Two of the most suggested tips are reading regularly and watching many movies.

 Today, we will explore other practical strategies that you can also start applying to expand your vocabulary.

CONTEXT CLUES

Context refers to the words and sentences that surround a particular word and help fix its meaning. 

It gives us hints in understanding an unfamiliar word. 

CONTEXT CLUES

Types of Context Clues:

There are four types of context clues that can help you:

  • Restatement
  • Positive/Negative
  • Contrast
  • Specific Detail

Callout

Determining Meaning from Context

Use the context found in the following sentences to determine the meaning of the italicized words. Highlight your answer. In your mind, try to identify the type of context clue you used to help you answer.

Karen was relieved to learn that the chemicals in her well water were all benign.

Benign means harmless. The sentence uses the positive/negative type of context. Karen’s feeling of relief would logically come from a positive thing, discovering that the chemicals are not toxic.

Although the film’s plot is admittedly trite, the characters are so endearing that the movie is highly entertaining despite the old storyline.

Trite means repeated too often. The example uses the restatement type of context. The main clue is the phrase “the old storyline,” which indicates that the movie’s plot is overused.

Ilona has such a caustic sense of humour that most people find his jokes upsetting rather than humorous.

Caustic means sarcastic. The main context clue is that people find Ilona’s jokes upsetting rather than humorous.

How much context do you need?

Sometimes a sentence does not provide the context you need to determine the meaning.

Take this sentence as an example: Sabina is an utterly inept dancer.

  • Is Sabina a graceful dancer? An awkward dance? Or an accomplished dancer? You simply cannot tell from the context, so you’ll have to apply other techniques in figuring out what “inept” means.

Prefixes & suffixes

When you come across unfamiliar words without context, breaking those words into their parts can help you determine their meaning. Good knowledge of prefixes and suffixes is essential to building your vocabulary. The more familiar you are with these fundamental word parts, the easier it will be to determine the meaning of unfamiliar words.

 

What are Prefixes & suffixes?

A prefix is a syllable attached to the beginning of a word to change or add to its meaning. A suffix is added to the end of a word. The base upon which prefixes are suffixes are added is called a root word.

Common prefixes

Here are some common prefixes and  their meanings:

Meaning

Again or back

Badly or wrongly

Common suffixes

Here are some common suffixes  and  their meanings:

Suffix

Meaning

Sample Words

-acy

Attached to a noun, it means state or quality

Privacy, fallacy, delicacy

-ate

Added to a verb, it means to become

Regulate, eradicate, enunciate

-able 

Attached to an adjective, it means capable of being 

Presentable, edible, credible

Take the time to memorize the most commons prefixes and suffixes. By remembering these essential word parts, you will be able to learn new words quickly. It will also be easier to decode the meaning of unfamiliar words. 

Synonyms and antonyms

The last strategy that we’re going to cover today is the use of antonyms and synonyms. 

What are synonyms and antonyms?

Synonyms are words that have the same meaning. Antonyms mean the opposite of another word.
