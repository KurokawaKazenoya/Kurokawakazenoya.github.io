Lesson 2 - Reading Strategies: Narrative Texts

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will focus on reading narrative texts. You will learn about dialogues and real-life narratives, two types of narrative texts. You will learn strategies to apply before, during, and after reading narrative texts to improve your reading comprehension. This lesson also recalls your knowledge of PACTS and relates its use to reading narrative texts.

Overall Expectations

A1. Demonstrate the ability to read and respond to a variety of texts.

A2. Demonstrate understanding of the organizational structure and features of a variety of informational, narrative, and graphic texts, including information paragraphs, opinion pieces, textbooks, newspaper reports magazine stories, and short fiction.

A3. Demonstrate understanding of the content and meaning of informational, narrative, and graphic texts that they have read using a variety of reading strategies.

Specific Expectations

A1.1 Demonstrate that they have read a variety of student-selected and teacher-selected texts, including informational, narrative, and graphic texts.

A1.2 Demonstrate the ability to read independently for personal, school-related, and career-related purposes.

A1.3 Describe, orally and/or in writing, personally relevant texts they have read and their responses to them.

A2.6 Use knowledge of the organizational structure of different types of non-fiction narratives (e.g., anecdote, recounting of events, biography, news report) and short fiction (e.g., dialogue, short story) to identify the sequence of events and main ideas.

A3.1 Choose an appropriate approach to reading a text to match the purpose of reading.

A3.2 Use appropriate pre-reading strategies to preview new texts.

A3.4 Use appropriate strategies to monitor comprehension when reading informational and narrative texts.

A3.5 Use appropriate strategies to track and record ideas and information while reading informational and narrative texts.

A3.8 Make connections between personal experiences and the content of texts to consolidate and extend understanding of different types of texts.

  

Learning Goals

  • Students will recognize the structure and purpose of a narrative text.
  • Students will learn about the two types of narrative texts included in the OSSLT.
  • Students will learn helpful strategies they can use before, during, and after reading narrative texts.
  • Students will apply the strategies they learned in answering questions about sample narrative texts.

    Success Criteria

    You know you are successful in the lesson if you can:

    • Recognize the structure of a sample narrative text.
    • Identify the purpose of the given narrative text.
    • Apply the strategies you learned before, during, and after reading narrative texts.
    • Answer the questions about the sample narrative text with accuracy.
