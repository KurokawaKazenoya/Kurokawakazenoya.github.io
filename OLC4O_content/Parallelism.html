Parallelism

Let’s define what a parallel structure is! Parallel structures are the use of the same pattern of words to emphasize the importance of each of the words. These are words that use the same form, and verb tenses should always match each word.

  • This can be a word, phrase, or clause.
  • For example,
  • Jonah loves talking, reading, and writing.
  • Error: Jonah loves to talk, reading, and writing.

Remember: 

A parallel structure must always be used when verb tenses are involved and verb tenses must always match each other.

You use a parallel structure when using coordinating conjunctions, and correlative conjunctions to connect two phrases or clauses.

When using coordinating conjunctions to connect two or more phrases or clauses, (e.g. for, and, but, nor, or, yer, so).


In the sentence: My brother and I eat our breakfast, making our bed and walking to school.

For example,

  • My brother and I eat our breakfast, make our bed, and walk to school.

When using correlative conjunctions to connect two or more phrases or clauses, (e.g. not only…but also, either…or, neither…nor, if…then, etc.).

In this sentence: My friend not only loves to play video games but also watch e-sports. 

There are errors in using the parallel structure.

For example:

My friend not only loves playing video games, but he also loves watching e-sports.

Alternatively:
My friend loves not only to play video games but also to watch e-sports.

A parallel structure should also be used when dealing with infinitives (base form of a verb) and words ending in -ing.

  • For infinitives,
  • Adam likes to walk, to run, and to play.
  • For words ending in -ing,
  • Eve loves walking, running, and playing

When writing clauses, parallel structures should also be used.
A parallel structure that starts with a clause or phrase, changing the form of the verb or using another pattern will break the parallelism and would make the sentence incorrect.

For example:
My friends told me that they were going to eat after the match, that they were going to take a bath after, and that they were going to sleep right away.
