Welcome to Unit 3

Unit 3 is a comprehensive exploration of language structure, narrative reading, and summarization skills. In the first lesson, participants learn to identify the antecedent of pronouns and understand the nuances of different personal pronouns. The focus is on applying correct pronoun-antecedent agreement rules, alongside insights into articles. Additionally, strategies for editing written work are provided, culminating in a practical application through journal work. This lesson not only strengthens participants' grasp of pronoun usage but also enhances their overall writing and editing proficiency.

The unit seamlessly transitions to narrative reading in the second lesson, where participants delve into dialogues and real-life narratives—two distinct types of narrative texts. Strategies for optimizing comprehension before, during, and after reading narrative texts are imparted. The lesson draws connections between these strategies and the previously learned PACTS framework, enriching participants' understanding of narrative text analysis.

The final lesson centers on summarization skills, covering the basics of crafting a good summary and its purpose. Participants are guided through the steps of summarizing text to improve comprehension. The practical application involves writing summaries for sample texts like "The State of Student Writing" and "Trapped in a Car for Five Days," refining participants' ability to distill essential information effectively. Unit 3 collectively equips participants with enhanced language awareness, narrative reading strategies, and summarization proficiency for comprehensive communication and analysis.
