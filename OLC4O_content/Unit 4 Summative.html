Building Writing Skills

B1 Demonstrate the ability to use the writing process by generating and organizing ideas and producing first drafts, revised drafts, and final polished pieces to complete a variety of writing tasks


B2 Use knowledge of writing forms and of the connections between form, audience, and purpose, to write summaries, information paragraphs, opinion pieces.



Ontario Curriculum Expectations

B 1.1 

identify the topic, the audience, the purpose for writing, and the requirements of the particular writing form

B 1.2

use prewriting strategies to generate ideas for writing

B 1.4

use appropriate strategies for gathering supporting ideas and information from print and electronic sources

B 1.5

use appropriate strategies to organize ideas and information for writing

B 1.6

create a first draft that includes the main and supporting ideas in the required form

B 1.7

revise drafts to ensure that ideas are presented in a logical order, to discard irrelevant ideas and information, to add details where information is insufficient, and to ensure a tone and level of language appropriate to the audience and purpose, using appropriate strategies

B 1.9

use appropriate strategies to edit written work to achieve accuracy in the use of the conventions of standard Canadian English, including the requirements of grammar, usage, spelling, and punctuation

B 2.4

construct clear, complete information paragraphs, some with graphic elements, for a variety of purposes, using correct paragraph structure

B 2.6

construct a series of paragraphs, clearly stating an opinion in the opening paragraph and supporting it in subsequent paragraphs with clear, sufficient, and convincing reasons

B 2.7

use knowledge of how to write summaries, information paragraphs, and opinion pieces to produce informational writing for a variety of personal and school-related purposes

B 2.9

construct clear, coherent, and objective news reports that include relevant facts, information, and supporting details, using the five-questions model – Who? What? Where? When? Why?

B 2.10

use knowledge of how to write news reports to create narratives related to other subject areas and personal interests

B 2.12

construct personal reflections, in paragraph form, choosing a clear focus and using appropriate examples to explain their thinking



Instructions: 

Part One: News Report Writing

Instructions:

Write the news report referred to below

  • You will need to both review and make up relevant details, including who, what, where, when, why, and how.

  • Include a relevant picture.

  • Remember: you only know what the staff of the newspaper know.

News Report Writing 1

  • Purpose: To write a factual and interesting account of the missing aircraft, aviator or pilot and 2 passengers.

  • Audience: Adults who read the local newspaper.

  • Length: The news report should be at least 150 words.

Outline:

Who?

What?

Where?

When?

Why?

How?

Supporting Details

Part Two: Opinion Writing

Write an opinion essay consisting of one introductory paragraph, one body paragraph and a concluding paragraph.



Opinion Essay 1

Topic: Many parents give children a weekly or monthly allowance regardless of their behavior because they believe an allowance teaches children to be financially responsible. Other parents only give children an allowance as a reward for completing chores or when they have behaved properly. Explain what you think parents should do and why.

Audience: An adult who is interested in your opinion



Insert your opinion essay here…

Opinion Essay 2

Topic: Give a habit that is harmful to their physical, emotional, or financial health and write an opinion piece to convince your reader to break this habit.

Audience: A fellow high school student

Knowledge: How well did you understand the different writing tasks (writing summaries, informative texts, news reports and series of paragraphs expressing opinion) and the instructions in each task? 

  • Insufficient or incomplete

  • shows a limited understanding of the different writing tasks (writing summaries, informative texts, news reports and series of paragraphs expressing opinion) and instructions in each task through providing relevant and precise details in his/her own news report.

  • shows adequate understanding of the different writing tasks (writing summaries, informative texts, news reports and series of paragraphs expressing opinion) and instructions in each task through providing relevant and precise details in his/her own news report.

  • shows considerable understanding of the different writing tasks (writing summaries, informative texts, news reports and series of paragraphs expressing opinion) and instructions in each task through providing relevant and precise details in his/her own news report.

  • shows a thorough understanding of the different writing tasks (writing summaries, informative texts, news reports and series of paragraphs expressing opinion) and instructions in each task through providing relevant and precise details in his/her own news report.

Thinking: How well did you make inferences, connect ideas, integrate personal knowledge to complete the different writing tasks (writing summaries, informative texts, news reports and series of paragraphs expressing opinion)? 

  • Insufficient or incomplete

  • demonstrates a limited competence in using critical and creative thinking skills through the presentation of the ideas in the report.

  • demonstrates moderate competence in using critical and creative thinking skills  through the presentation of the ideas in the report.

  • demonstrates considerable competence in using critical and creative thinking skills through the presentation of the ideas in the report.

  • demonstrates a high degree of competence in using critical and creative thinking skills through the presentation of the ideas in the report.

Communication: How well did you communicate your responses to the different writing tasks (writing summaries, informative texts, news reports and series of paragraphs expressing opinion)?

  • Insufficient or incomplete

  • Communicates ideas and information with limited clarity and persuasion for different audiences.

  • Communicates ideas and information with moderate clarity and persuasion for different audiences.

  • Communicates ideas and information with considerable clarity and persuasion for different audiences.

  • Communicates ideas and information with a high degree of clarity and persuasion for different audiences.

Application: How well did you apply required language conventions in your responses to the different writing tasks (writing summaries, informative texts, news reports and series of paragraphs expressing opinion)?

  • Insufficient or incomplete

  • Uses the required language conventions with limited accuracy and effectiveness. 

  • Uses the required conventions with a moderate degree of accuracy and effectiveness. 

  • Uses the required language conventions with considerable accuracy and effectiveness. 

  • Uses the required language conventions with a high degree of accuracy and effectiveness.
