Writing Summaries  

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will learn the basics of a good summary and its purpose. You will also be guided with the steps to summarize text and improve comprehension.

Finally, you will practice writing summaries on sample texts like “The State of Student Writing” and “Trapped in a Car for Five Days”.

Overall Expectations

B1 Demonstrate the ability to use the writing process by generating and organizing ideas and producing first drafts, revised drafts, and final polished pieces to complete a variety of writing tasks.

Specific Expectations

B 1.9 Use appropriate strategies to edit written work to achieve accuracy in the use of the conventions of standard Canadian English, including the requirements of grammar, usage, spelling, and punctuation listed below: write complete and correct sentences.

Success Criteria

You know you are successful in the lesson if you can:

  • Define a parallel structure.
  • Use the correct parallel structure in the quiz and in their writing.
  • Learn about run-on sentences and its rules.
  • Apply the rules of run-on sentences in the quiz and in their writing.
