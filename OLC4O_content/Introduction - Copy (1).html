Lesson 2 - Parts of Speech

Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will go over the eight parts of speech, learn about the tenses of the verb, learn about correct subject-verb agreement, learn how to apply the subject-verb agreement rules in constructing grammatically correct sentences and learn about transition words and phrases. At the end of the lesson, you will learn how to use the transition words and phrases in a sentence. 

Overall Expectations

B1 Demonstrate the ability to use the writing process by generating and organizing ideas and producing first drafts, revised drafts, and final polished pieces to complete a variety of writing tasks.

B3 Editing, Proofreading, and Publishing: use editing, proofreading, and publishing skills and strategies to refine and polish their work.

Specific Expectations

B1.1 Identify the topic, the audience, the purpose for writing, and the requirements of the particular writing form.

B1.9 use appropriate strategies to edit written work (e.g., read aloud to detect errors; correct errors using personal checklists of “look-fors”, a shared word/grammar wall, a personal grammar guide, and/or computer spelling and grammar programs) to achieve accuracy in the use of the conventions of standard Canadian English, including the requirements of grammar, usage, spelling, and punctuation listed below.

    

Grammar and Usage Spelling Punctuation

• write complete and correct sentences

• use consistent and appropriate verb tense and voice

• make verbs agree with subjects

• make pronouns agree with their antecedents in number and gender

• use knowledge of spelling patterns and rules, and a variety of appropriate resources, to spell correctly (e.g., personal dictionaries, class word walls, sound-based and visual spelling strategies)

• use punctuation correctly, including period, question mark, exclamation mark, comma, dash, apostrophe, colon, quotation marks, parentheses, and ellipses

Learning Goals

  • Students will learn about the parts of speech and verb tenses.

  • Students will identify when to use different parts of speech and verb tenses.

  • Students will use appropriate strategies to edit written work to achieve accuracy in the use of the conventions of standard Canadian English including the requirements of grammar: use consistent and appropriate verb tense and voice.

  • Students will edit and proofread their written work, applying the conventions of transition words and phrases appropriately and effectively.

    Success Criteria

    You know you are successful in the lesson if you can:

    • Identify different parts of speech and verb tenses.

    • Apply correct parts of speech and verb tenses in your writing and assessments.

    • Use appropriate strategies to edit written work to achieve accuracy in the use of the conventions of standard Canadian English including the requirements of grammar: use consistent and appropriate verb tense and voice.

    • Edit and proofread their written work, applying the conventions of transition words and phrases appropriately and effectively.
