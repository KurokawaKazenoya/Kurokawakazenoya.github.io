  • To solve an exponential equation algebraically, take the logarithm of both sides of the equation using a base of 10, and then use the power rule for logarithms to simplify the equation and solve for the unknown.
  • Sometimes an exponential equation can be solved algebraically by writing both sides of the equation with the same base (if possible), setting the exponents equal to each other, and solving for the unknown.
  • Exponential equations can also be solved with graphing technology, using the same strategies that are used for other kinds of equations.
