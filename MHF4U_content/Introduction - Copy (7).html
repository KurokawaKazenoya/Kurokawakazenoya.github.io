Lesson Description

Explore the characteristics of new functions created by combining functions.

Represent the sums and differences of two functions graphically and algebraically and determine their properties.

Overall Expectations

D2. Determine functions that result from the addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division of two functions and from the composition of two functions, describe some properties of the resulting functions, and solve related problems.

Specific Expectations

D2.1 Determine, through investigation using graphing technology, key features (e.g., domain, range, maximum/minimum points, number of zeros) of the graphs of functions created by adding, subtracting, multiplying, or dividing functions [e.g., f(x) =&nbsp;2-x sin 4x, g(x) = x2 + 2x, h(x) = sin x/cos x], and describe factors that affect these properties. 

Learning Goals

  • Students will explore the characteristics of new functions created by combining functions.

  • Students can represent the sums and differences of two functions graphically and algebraically and determine their properties.

Lesson Description

Solve equations and inequalities that involve combinations of functions using a variety of techniques. 

Overall Expectations

C4. Demonstrate an understanding of solving polynomial and simple rational inequalities.

Specific Expectations

C4.2 determines solutions to polynomial inequalities in one variable [e.g., solve f(x) ≥ 0, where f(x) = x3 – x2 + 3x – 9] and to simple rational inequalities in one variable by graphing the corresponding functions, using graphing technology, and identifying intervals for which x satisfies the inequalities.

Learning Goals

  • Students can solve equations and inequalities that involve combinations of functions using a variety of techniques.
