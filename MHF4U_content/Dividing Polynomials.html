Dividing Polynomials

Synthetic Division

Notes on synthetic division:

  1. The divisor must be in the form (x-k)
  2. All terms must be represented, even if they have a coefficient of zero
  3. If the root, k, is a fraction, you may prefer long division to reduce the risk of an error.
  4. If the remainder of the division is zero, then both the quotient and the divisor are factors of the original polynomial.
