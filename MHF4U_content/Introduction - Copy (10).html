Lesson Description

Investigate the inverse of the exponential function.

Determine the effects of varying the parameters of the graph of y= alog10 (k(x-d))+c.

Overall Expectations

A1. demonstrate an understanding of the relationship between exponential expressions and logarithmic expressions, evaluate logarithms, and apply the laws of logarithms to simplify numeric expressions.

Specific Expectations

A1.1 recognize the logarithm of a number to a given base as the exponent to which the base must be raised to get the number, recognize the operation of finding the logarithm to be the inverse operation (i.e., the undoing or reversing) of exponentiation, and evaluate simple logarithmic expressions.

Learning Goals

  • Students will investigate the inverse of the exponential function.

  • Students will determine the effects of varying the parameters of the graph of y= a log10(k(x-d))+c.

Lesson Description

Evaluate logarithmic expressions and approximate the logarithm of a number to any base.

Recognize the connection between the laws of exponents and the laws of logarithms and use the laws of logarithms to simplify expressions.

Overall Expectations

A1. Demonstrate an understanding of the relationship between exponential expressions and logarithmic expressions, evaluate logarithms, and apply the laws of logarithms to simplify numeric expressions.

Specific Expectations

A1.2 determine, with technology, the approximate logarithm of a number to any base, including base 10 (e.g., by reasoning that log329 is between 3 and 4 and using systematic trial to determine that log329 is approximately 3.07).

Learning Goals

  • Students can evaluate logarithmic expressions and approximate the logarithm of a number to any base.

  • Students can recognize the connection between the laws of exponents and the laws of logarithms and use the laws of logarithms to simplify expressions.

Lesson Description

Solve exponential equations in one variable using a variety of strategies.

Overall Expectations

A3. solve exponential and simple logarithmic equations in one variable algebraically, including those in problems arising from real-world applications.

Specific Expectations

3.2 solve exponential equations in one variable by determining a common base (e.g., solve 4x = 8x+3 by expressing each side as a power of 2) and by using logarithms (e.g., solve 4x = 8x+3 by taking the logarithm base 2 of both sides), recognizing that logarithms base 10 are commonly used (e.g., solving 3x = 7 by taking the logarithm base 10 of both sides). 

Learning Goals

  • Students can solve exponential equations in one variable using a variety of strategies.
