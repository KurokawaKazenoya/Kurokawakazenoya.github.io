Solving Polynomial Equations

When we are asked to “solve an equation”, this means we are looking to find the “real roots” of an equation.

For quadratics, we can solve by

  • factoring to find the zeros (roots)
  • graphing
  • completing the square (vertex form) and solving for y=0
  • quadratic formula

If we have a polynomial with a degree of 3 or higher we can

  • graph
  • factor down to degree 2 (quadratic), then apply one of the techniques listed above.

Steps to Solving Polynomial Equation

(1) Rearrange the equation. Set equal to zero

(2) Apply the factor theorem.

(3) Factor out the first term (polynomial division) and repeat until in a fully factored form.

(4) Find the roots of the equation (i.e., set it back to zero and solve).

(5) Think about the domain. Ignore solutions that are outside of the domain. This is defined by the conditions of the problem.
