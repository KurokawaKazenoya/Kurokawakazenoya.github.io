Exploring Equivalent Trigonometric Functions

Because of their periodic nature, there are many equivalent trigonometric expressions.

Two expressions may be equivalent if the graphs created by a graphing calculator of their corresponding functions coincide, producing only one visible graph over the entire domain of both functions. To demonstrate equivalency requires additional reasoning about the properties of both graphs.

You can identify equivalent trigonometric expressions by comparing principal angles drawn in standard position in quadrants II, III, and IV with their related acute angle Ꝋ, in quadrant I.
