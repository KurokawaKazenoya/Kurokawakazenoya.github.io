Secant Lines

The formula for an average rate of change is the same as the slope of a straight line.  So, we can use a secant line to determine the average rate of change between two points in a graph.

A secant line is a line that passes through two points on the graph of a relation.

Since f(x) is not linear, the actual rate of change between P and Q may be different at every point.  However, we can use a secant line to determine the average rate of change between P and Q.
