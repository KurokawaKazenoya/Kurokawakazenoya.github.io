Rates of Change 

The rate of change describes how one variable changes concerning another variable. In the context of functions and graphs, it specifically refers to how the output of a function changes as the input variable changes.

Slope 

The slope represents the "rise" (vertical change) over the "run" (horizontal change) between two points. A positive slope indicates an upward direction, while a negative slope indicates a downward direction. A slope of zero represents a horizontal line, and an undefined slope represents a vertical line.

Average Rate of Change 

The average rate of change for any function f(x) over the interval x1 ≤ x ≤ x2 is equivalent to the slope of the secant line passing through two points (x1,y1) and (x2,y2).
