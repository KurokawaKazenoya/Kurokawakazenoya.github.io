Secant Lines

 

Callout

Clearly, the formula for average rate of change is the same formula as the slope of a straight line.  So we can use a secant line to determine the average rate of change between two points in a graph.

A secant line is a line that passes through two points on the graph of a relation.

Since f(x) is not linear, the actual rate of change between P and Q may be different at every point.  However, we can use a secant line to determine the average rate of change between P and Q.

 

 

The average rate of change of a function is the change in the quantity represented by the dependent variable Δy divided by the corresponding change in the quantity represented by the independent variable Δx over an interval x1 ≤ x ≤ x2 .
